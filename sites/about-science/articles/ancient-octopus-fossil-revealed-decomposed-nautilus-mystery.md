# Ancient 'Octopus' Fossil Revealed to Be Decomposed Nautilus After 26 Years of Mystery

![A Paleocadmus nautilus decaying on ancient seafloor](https://www.sciencenews.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/04/040726_JB_octopus_main.jpg)

A groundbreaking scientific revelation has turned paleontology on its head: what researchers believed for over two decades to be the world's oldest octopus fossil is actually the remains of an entirely different creature. The dramatic discovery, published in the prestigious Proceedings of the Royal Society B, fundamentally reshapes our understanding of how cephalopods—the intelligent group including octopuses, squids, and nautiluses—evolved over hundreds of millions of years.

## The Mystery That Plagued Scientists for Decades

The story begins in 2000, when researchers unearthed a peculiar fossil near Chicago that would spark scientific debate for years to come. The 310-million-year-old specimen, dubbed *Pohlsepia mazonensis*, appeared to possess the telltale features of an octopus: a rounded body, fin-like appendages, and what seemed to be a tangle of arms. However, its extraordinary age created a perplexing evolutionary puzzle that left paleontologists scratching their heads.

"It's been a real trouble for paleontologists to try to understand how Pohlsepia fits into our understanding of octopus evolution," explains Thomas Clements, a paleontologist at the University of Reading in England who led the groundbreaking reinvestigation.

The problem was timing. While octopus fossils are well-documented in the geological record, they don't appear until at least 150 million years ago—a staggering 160-million-year gap that suggested either a massive missing chapter in evolutionary history or a fundamental misidentification.

## Revolutionary X-Ray Technology Unveils Hidden Secrets

Determined to solve this paleontological mystery, Clements and his international team employed cutting-edge synchrotron X-ray technology to peer deeper into the fossil than ever before. This advanced technique illuminated the chemical composition of preserved minerals that had formed around the ancient creature's soft tissues, revealing details invisible to conventional examination methods.

The breakthrough moment came when the high-powered X-rays revealed a crucial anatomical feature that had been hidden for millions of years: a preserved radula, the rasping tongue found in many mollusks. This discovery would prove to be the smoking gun that solved the decades-old mystery.

"That was the big breakthrough," Clements emphasizes. "Because it's the only unequivocal character this fossil has."

![A shell-like brownish fossil on black background](https://www.sciencenews.org/wp-content/uploads/sites/2/2026/04/040726_JB_octopus_inline_17ea58.jpg)

## The Telltale Teeth That Rewrote History

The radula's structure provided irrefutable evidence of the creature's true identity. Unlike octopuses, which possess seven or nine teeth per row on their radulae, the *Pohlsepia* specimen displayed at least eleven teeth—a configuration that perfectly matches nautiluses, those ancient shelled cephalopods that still glide through modern oceans as "living fossils."

Even more compelling, the teeth closely resembled those found on fossilized radulae belonging to *Paleocadmus pohli*, an extinct nautilus species discovered at the same Illinois fossil site. This remarkable correlation led Clements and his team to conclude that *Pohlsepia* was actually a misidentified *Paleocadmus* specimen.

## Why the Confusion? The Power of Decay

The misidentification becomes understandable when considering the dramatic effects of decomposition on soft-bodied marine creatures. The research team believes the nautilus had begun rotting before fossilization, fundamentally altering its appearance and causing it to lose its characteristic shell—a process observed in modern dead nautiluses.

"There are examples of dead modern nautiluses separating from their shells as they decayed," Clements notes, explaining how the decomposition process could have created the octopus-like appearance that fooled researchers for over two decades.

## Scientific Community Celebrates the Resolution

The paleontological community has welcomed this resolution to a long-standing controversy with enthusiasm. Alexander Pohle, a paleontologist at Ruhr University Bochum in Germany who was not involved in the study, expressed his satisfaction with the findings.

"There had been serious doubts about the alleged octopod identity of Pohlsepia for some time," Pohle states. "It's great to see this debate settled with such detailed work!"

## Implications for Evolutionary Understanding

This reclassification carries profound implications for our understanding of cephalopod evolution. The removal of *Pohlsepia* from the octopus lineage means that octopuses as a group are significantly younger than previously thought—eliminating the need to explain how cephalopods could have evolved so early in mollusk evolutionary history.

The discovery effectively relaxes the evolutionary timeline for these remarkable creatures, providing a more coherent picture of how intelligence and complex behaviors developed in marine environments over geological time.

## The Future of Fossil Investigation

As technology continues advancing at breakneck speed, Clements anticipates that even more secrets may emerge from this enigmatic fossil. The possibility of future technological breakthroughs offers hope for resolving remaining questions about ancient life.

"Maybe in 10 or 20 years' time, a new piece of kit will come along and someone will zap Pohlsepia again and be like, 'Oh, we can now definitely work out what this thing is,'" he speculates, highlighting the dynamic nature of paleontological discovery.

## A Testament to Scientific Progress

This remarkable case demonstrates how scientific understanding evolves through persistent investigation and technological advancement. What seemed like a clear-cut discovery in 2000 has been thoroughly re-examined and reinterpreted, showcasing the self-correcting nature of scientific inquiry.

The *Pohlsepia* revelation serves as a powerful reminder that in paleontology, as in all sciences, our understanding of the ancient world continues to evolve with each new discovery and technological breakthrough. As researchers continue probing the mysteries of deep time, who knows what other surprising revelations await in the fossil record?
