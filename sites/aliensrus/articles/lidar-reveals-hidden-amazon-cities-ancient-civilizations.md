## Technological Revolution in Archaeology

LIDAR technology represents a paradigm shift in archaeological methodology. By using laser pulses to create detailed topographical maps, researchers can identify structural remains and landscape modifications that are invisible to traditional survey methods. This capability is particularly valuable in heavily vegetated areas where conventional archaeological techniques face significant limitations.

The Amazon discoveries demonstrate how this technology is democratizing access to archaeological sites that were previously beyond reach. Remote sensing allows researchers to identify potential excavation sites more efficiently and to understand broader settlement patterns across vast landscapes.
