As students in East Brunswick prepare to head back to school, ensuring their safety on the roads is paramount. The East Brunswick Police Department is actively promoting safety guidelines for students, parents, and drivers. These measures aim to create a secure environment for everyone involved in the daily commute to and from school.

## Safety Tips for Students

Children walking to school should always use sidewalks and crosswalks. It's crucial for them to look both ways before crossing streets and to remain vigilant of their surroundings. Students are encouraged to stay off their phones while walking, as distractions can lead to accidents. Wearing brightly colored clothing can also enhance visibility for drivers.

## Guidelines for Parents

Parents play a significant role in ensuring their children's safety. Teaching children about the importance of traffic signals and signs is essential. Additionally, parents should arrange a buddy system, where children walk with friends or siblings, to increase safety. For those driving their children to school, using designated drop-off and pick-up zones helps maintain an orderly flow of traffic around school premises.

## Driver Responsibilities

Drivers must exercise caution, especially in school zones. Adhering to speed limits and being alert for stopping school buses is vital. In New Jersey, it is mandatory for drivers to stop at least 25 feet away from a school bus with flashing red lights. This law is in place to protect students boarding or alighting from buses. Failing to comply can lead to severe penalties.

### School Bus Safety

School buses are designed with safety in mind, but students need to follow rules to ensure their safety. They should remain seated while the bus is in motion and avoid distracting the driver. It's important for students to wait for the bus to come to a complete stop before approaching it. Parents should remind children to be cautious around buses and to never walk behind them.

## Community Involvement

The East Brunswick community is encouraged to participate in creating a safer environment for students. Local schools often hold safety seminars and workshops, which provide valuable information to parents and children. These initiatives foster a sense of community responsibility and awareness.

As the new school year begins, focusing on safety is crucial for students, parents, and drivers alike. By following these guidelines, the East Brunswick community can ensure a safer and more efficient route to education. Cooperation and vigilance will help protect our children as they embark on another academic journey.
