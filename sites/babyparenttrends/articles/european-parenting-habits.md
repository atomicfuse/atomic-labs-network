## Discovering European Parenting Habits

During a recent trip, Dr. Sukhmani Gumber, an Indian mother and doctor, observed several European parenting habits that stood out to her. With modern parenting pressures often requiring us to entertain and optimize children's experiences, these five habits offered a refreshing perspective. Adopting some of these strategies might foster more resilient and independent children.

## 1. Your Child Doesn't Have to Be the Center of Your World

A key takeaway from European parenting is the ability to let children understand that life isn’t solely about them. It's okay for parents to have their interests and social lives apart from family time. By not making kids the focal point of every activity, parents encourage them to engage with the world around them, fostering independence and social skills. This adjustment can significantly relieve parental burnout while also instilling a sense of self-reliance in children.

## 2. Boredom Isn’t That Bad

In Europe, children experience boredom as part of their everyday lives without constant stimulation. This boredom encourages kids to think creatively and develop their imagination. Instead of immediately intervening or providing toys or screens, allow your child to experience a little downtime. It’s during these moments that they can discover new interests and learn to entertain themselves — skills that are invaluable as they grow older.

## 3. Table Manners Can Start Younger Than We Think

Dr. Gumber noted that European parents often teach their children table manners from a very young age. In her observations, kids were encouraged to sit at the table with adults, learning the social cues of dining through participation rather than simply being told what to do. Start small by involving your child in mealtime conversations and expectations, even if they’re still toddlers. It builds their confidence and helps them feel included in family dynamics.

## 4. Kids Can Eat What the Rest of the Family Eats

Another fascinating element of European parenting is the practice of children eating the same meals as their families, instead of being catered to with separate, kid-friendly options. This habit not only promotes healthier eating choices but also teaches children about food diversity. When kids are included in mealtime decisions and exposed to a variety of cuisines, they are more likely to develop an adventurous palate and a healthier relationship with food.

## 5. Don’t Panic Over Public Tantrums

In many European cultures, parents stay calm during their children’s public tantrums, viewing these moments as natural and a part of emotional development. Instead of reacting with stress or embarrassment, they approach the situation with understanding. This technique helps children learn to manage their emotions, and parents cultivate resilience as they handle each situation with grace. So next time you're in public and your little one starts to tantrum, try taking a breath and manage the moment with calmness rather than panic.

## Final Thoughts

Integrating these five European parenting habits into your daily life can help you transition toward a more relaxed and resilient parenting style. Begin with one or two of these habits to see how they resonate with your family. By allowing children to experience the world around them, fostering creativity through boredom, and encouraging independence at mealtimes and during emotional outbursts, you can pave the way for a confident and adaptable child. Embrace the idea that a little distance can lead to greater strength for both you and your child.
