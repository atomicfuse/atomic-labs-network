## Reality TV Meets Real Politics

Spencer Pratt's unexpected foray into Los Angeles politics has come to an end after the reality TV personality failed to advance to the November mayoral runoff. According to reports, Pratt finished third in the June 2026 primary election, trailing behind incumbent Mayor Karen Bass and City Councilmember Nithya Raman.

## From Fire Victim to Political Candidate

Pratt's political ambitions emerged from personal tragedy when the 2025 Palisades Fire destroyed his home. The devastating loss apparently served as a catalyst for his entry into the mayoral race, positioning himself as an anti-establishment candidate focused on fighting what he characterized as systemic corruption within city government.

The campaign represented a dramatic shift for the former "Hills" star, who leveraged his personal disaster experience as the foundation of his political platform. Reports indicate that Pratt framed his candidacy around accountability and disaster response issues that directly affected his own life.

## Celebrity Status Versus Electoral Reality

Despite initial momentum and significant media attention, Pratt's campaign highlighted the persistent gap between celebrity name recognition and actual electoral viability. This strategic positioning may have been designed to appeal to Los Angeles voters who typically lean Democratic, while maintaining support from his conservative base. However, the approach ultimately proved insufficient to secure a spot in the November runoff.
