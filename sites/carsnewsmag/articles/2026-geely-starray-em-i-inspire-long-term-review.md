The Geely Starray EM-i Inspire arrives with a clear mission: deliver plug-in hybrid efficiency, family-friendly space and premium-style equipment without pushing into luxury SUV pricing. For Australian buyers comparing new medium SUVs, this electrified newcomer is one of the more interesting options to watch.

## Geely Starray EM-i Inspire: a fresh plug-in hybrid family SUV

Geely may still be building its local profile, but the company is far from a small player globally. The wider Geely group has links to major brands including Volvo, Polestar and Lotus, which gives this new SUV a stronger background than some buyers may expect.

The Starray EM-i sits in the popular medium SUV class. It uses a plug-in hybrid system, so it can cover many daily trips on electric power while still carrying a petrol engine for longer drives. That combination is becoming increasingly attractive for families not ready to move to a full EV.

This review focuses on the Inspire grade, which is the higher-spec version of the Starray EM-i. It aims to tempt buyers with a long equipment list, modern cabin technology and a quieter, more efficient driving experience than a traditional petrol SUV.

## Powertrain and efficiency: why the EM-i system matters

The key selling point is Geely's EM-i plug-in hybrid drivetrain. It combines a 1.5-litre petrol engine with an electric motor and a lithium battery pack. The system is designed to prioritise electric running in town, then use petrol power when the battery charge drops or higher-speed driving demands it.

For families, this setup can make a lot of sense. School runs, shopping trips and short commutes may often be completed without using much fuel. Longer weekend drives are less stressful because the petrol engine removes range anxiety.

The Starray EM-i also supports external charging. Owners who can plug in at home overnight will get the most from the package. Public charging can add extra flexibility, although the real benefit comes from regular low-cost charging at home.

Official figures for plug-in hybrids can vary widely in real-world use. They depend heavily on charging habits, trip length, weather and driving style. Over a longer test, the main question is not just how efficient the Starray can be, but how easy it is to keep it operating at its best.

## First impressions from behind the wheel

In daily driving, the Starray EM-i Inspire feels most convincing when it is operating as an electric SUV. It moves away smoothly, keeps noise levels low and suits suburban traffic well. That relaxed character is important in a family car, where calm progress often matters more than outright speed.

The electric motor gives the SUV useful response when leaving intersections or merging into traffic. The petrol engine can join in when needed, but the best moments come when the system remains quiet and seamless.

Ride comfort is also a key part of the ownership experience. The Inspire grade brings a more upmarket look, and larger wheels help its stance. However, wheel size can affect how settled a car feels over sharp bumps. That will be worth watching across rough suburban streets, speed humps and country roads.

Steering effort is light enough for parking and city use. That suits the Starray's role as a family SUV. It does not need to feel sporty to be successful. It needs to be easy, predictable and comfortable across repeated everyday trips.

## Interior design and cabin technology

Inside, the Starray EM-i Inspire leans heavily into a modern digital layout. A large central touchscreen manages infotainment and many vehicle settings, while a digital driver display gives the cabin a contemporary feel.

The presentation is one of the Geely's strongest first impressions. The dashboard design looks clean, the materials feel more ambitious than expected at this price point, and the cabin avoids the plain look found in some value-focused SUVs.

High-grade equipment is an important part of the Inspire's appeal. Features commonly expected by family buyers — such as smartphone connectivity, climate control, a panoramic-style glass roof, powered front seats and advanced camera assistance — help it feel well stocked.

There is a trade-off, though. Like many new SUVs, the Starray places several functions inside the touchscreen. That gives the cabin a neat appearance, but physical buttons are often easier while driving. Over time, menu layout and software responsiveness will become just as important as screen size.

## Family practicality: space, storage and child-seat use

A family SUV has to do more than look advanced. It needs to handle bags, bottles, school gear, prams, sports equipment and passengers without constant compromise. On that front, the Starray EM-i starts with the advantage of a roomy five-seat layout.

Rear-seat space is generous enough for children and adults, with good legroom and a bright cabin feel. Wide-opening doors should make it easier to load kids or fit child restraints. ISOFIX points on the outboard rear seats and top-tether anchors provide the installation points parents expect.

The boot is another important test. The Starray offers a useful cargo area for grocery runs and weekend packing, although plug-in hybrid hardware can sometimes affect underfloor storage. Families should check how the space works with their own pram, luggage or sporting needs.

Cabin storage appears well considered, with areas for phones, drink bottles and everyday clutter. Small details matter here. A family car can feel frustrating very quickly if there is nowhere practical to put keys, snacks, cables or school notes.

## Safety technology and driver assistance

Modern Chinese SUVs often arrive with a long list of safety and driver-assistance features, and the Starray EM-i Inspire follows that trend. Expected systems include autonomous emergency braking, lane support, blind-spot monitoring, rear cross-traffic assistance, adaptive cruise control and a surround-view camera.

These features can be genuinely helpful, especially in a vehicle used for family transport. Clear camera views simplify parking near schools and shopping centres. Blind-spot assistance is useful on busy multi-lane roads. Adaptive cruise control can reduce fatigue during longer highway trips.

The challenge is tuning. Safety alerts need to be accurate and well judged. If warnings become too sensitive, drivers may find them distracting. A strong long-term review should pay close attention to how the systems behave in normal Australian conditions.

## Value against key plug-in hybrid SUV rivals

The Starray EM-i enters a segment that is getting more competitive. Buyers may compare it with models such as the BYD Sealion 6, Mitsubishi Outlander Plug-in Hybrid, Chery Tiggo 7 Super Hybrid and conventional hybrid SUVs from Toyota, Kia and Hyundai.

Geely's strongest argument is value. The Inspire grade offers a premium-style feature set while remaining more accessible than many established plug-in hybrid rivals. That matters because families often need to balance purchase price, running costs, warranty cover and resale confidence.

Warranty coverage and ownership support will play a major role in buyer confidence. Newer brands need to prove that servicing, parts supply and dealer backup can match their attractive specifications. A generous warranty helps, but the real test comes after months and years of ownership.

## What to watch during a long-term test

The first stage of testing is about impressions. The important answers come later. A plug-in hybrid can look excellent on paper, but ownership depends on everyday habits and real-world usability.

Key areas to monitor include fuel use with regular charging, fuel use without charging, charging speed, infotainment reliability and how the cabin handles family wear. Tyre noise, ride comfort, safety alert tuning and boot usability will also shape the final verdict.

It will also be important to see whether the Starray remains enjoyable once the novelty fades. Good long-term family cars make life easier without demanding constant thought. They become part of the routine, not another chore.

## Conclusion: a promising start for Geely's family-focused PHEV

The Geely Starray EM-i Inspire makes a strong early case as a practical plug-in hybrid SUV for Australian families. It offers efficient electric driving for short trips, petrol backup for longer travel, a spacious cabin and a generous equipment list.

Some questions remain. Real-world economy, software ease, ride quality and safety system calibration will decide whether it can become a genuine class standout. For now, it looks like a serious new contender in the growing plug-in hybrid family SUV market.
