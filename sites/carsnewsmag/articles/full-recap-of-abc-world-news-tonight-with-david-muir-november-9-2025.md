On the November 9, 2025 broadcast of ABC World News Tonight with David Muir, a number of significant stories were covered, each with impactful implications on both national and international levels.

The evening began with a focus on the prolonged government shutdown, now in its 40th day. Selina Wang reported on the potential deal that senators are reportedly finalizing, which could end the shutdown and offer relief to millions of Americans.

Thousands of flights have been delayed or canceled due to staffing shortages and decreased flight operations, prompting a stark warning from the Transportation Secretary. These issues underscore the widespread effects of the government shutdown on the daily lives of citizens.

Linsey Davis delivered an inspiring story in the America Strong segment, honoring Frances Masters, a 103-year-old Rosie the Riveter. Masters, who symbolizes resilience and dedication, was celebrated with her own statue, recognizing her contributions during and after World War II.

Other stories included a severe weather warning with millions bracing for the first arctic chill of the season, a violent confrontation in Chicago involving ICE agents, and a tragic street race in Tampa resulting in multiple fatalities and injuries. The broadcast also touched on international news, such as the displacement of one million people in the Philippines due to a deadly super typhoon.

In addition, the Justice Department is investigating meatpacking companies, and a recall of baby formula linked to botulism has been issued. Finally, a touching story of an 86-year-old skydiver making her 1,000th jump concluded the broadcast on a high note.

Overall, this episode of ABC World News Tonight with David Muir provided a comprehensive overview of current events, emphasizing both challenges and triumphs in today's world.
