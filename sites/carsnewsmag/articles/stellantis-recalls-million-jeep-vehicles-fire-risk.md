## Major Safety Recall Affects Popular Jeep Models

Stellantis has issued a significant recall affecting over 1 million Jeep vehicles due to a serious fire risk, according to reports. The recall encompasses Jeep Wrangler and Gladiator models from the 2021 through 2025 model years, making it one of the largest automotive recalls in recent memory.

The safety concern centers around an electrical defect in the power steering pump wiring that could potentially cause fires. This defect has prompted the automaker to take the unusual step of advising affected vehicle owners to park their Jeeps outdoors and away from buildings while they await repairs.

## Immediate Action Required for Owners

According to reports, Stellantis is urging owners of affected vehicles to take immediate precautionary measures. The company's guidance specifically recommends parking these vehicles outdoors and maintaining distance from structures until a proper repair solution becomes available.

The recall affects an exceptionally popular vehicle line during what is typically peak driving season, potentially impacting hundreds of thousands of drivers across the country. The timing of this recall adds urgency to the situation as summer travel plans may need to be adjusted for safety reasons.

## Repair Timeline and Availability

Stellantis has indicated that a repair solution should become available by July, according to reports. This timeline means affected owners will need to manage the inconvenience and safety concerns for several weeks while waiting for the fix to be developed and distributed to dealerships.

The extended timeline between the recall announcement and repair availability underscores the complexity of addressing electrical defects in modern vehicles. Power steering systems are critical safety components, and any modifications must be thoroughly tested before implementation.
