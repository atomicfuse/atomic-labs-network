## The Quest for Perfect Pakode

The combination of crispy pakode and chai represents one of food's most beloved pairings, especially during rainy weather and afternoon snack cravings. However, many home cooks struggle with achieving the perfect texture, often ending up with soggy, oil-heavy results instead of the light, crispy pakode found at street stalls.

MasterChef Pankaj Bhadouria recently addressed this common kitchen challenge by sharing five practical tips that can transform homemade moong dal pakode from disappointing to restaurant-quality. According to reports, these techniques focus on batter preparation, ingredient selection, and proper frying methods to achieve extra crispiness while reducing oil absorption.

## Five Essential Tips for Crispy Pakode

### Skip the Water, Perfect the Whisk

According to Chef Bhadouria's guidance, the first crucial step involves avoiding water in the batter preparation and ensuring thorough whisking. This technique appears to be fundamental to achieving the right batter consistency that leads to crispier results.

### Rice Flour Makes the Difference

The addition of rice flour emerges as a key ingredient for achieving the desired texture. This common pantry staple reportedly helps create the crispy exterior that distinguishes professional-quality pakode from home versions.

### Baking Soda for Fluffiness

Incorporating baking soda into the batter serves as another essential element in the chef's formula. This leavening agent contributes to creating fluffier, lighter pakode that maintain their texture rather than becoming dense or heavy.

### The Hot Oil Technique

One of the more unique tips involves adding hot oil to the batter itself. This technique reportedly helps achieve better texture and may contribute to reducing overall oil absorption during the frying process.

### Temperature Control is Critical

The final tip focuses on monitoring oil temperature carefully before frying. Proper temperature control ensures optimal cooking conditions that result in crispy exteriors without excessive oil penetration.
