## Expert Solutions for Common Pakode Problems

The beloved combination of crispy pakode and chai has long been a comfort food staple, particularly during rainy seasons and snack cravings. However, many home cooks struggle with achieving that perfect street-style texture, often ending up with soggy, oil-laden results instead of the desired crispy crunch.

MasterChef Pankaj Bhadouria recently addressed this common kitchen challenge by sharing five practical techniques that can transform homemade moong dal pakode from disappointing to delicious. According to reports, these chef-approved methods focus on batter preparation, ingredient selection, and proper frying techniques.

## The Five Essential Techniques

### Perfecting the Batter Base

The first crucial step involves avoiding water altogether and ensuring thorough whisking of the batter. This foundational technique helps create the proper consistency needed for crispy results, according to Chef Bhadouria's recommendations.

### Strategic Ingredient Additions

The addition of rice flour serves as a key ingredient for achieving superior texture. This pantry staple helps create the crispy exterior that distinguishes restaurant-quality pakode from their soggy homemade counterparts.

Baking soda also plays a vital role in the process, contributing to the fluffy, light texture that makes pakode so appealing. The chemical reaction helps create air pockets that result in better crunch.

### The Hot Oil Method

One particularly innovative technique involves adding hot oil directly to the batter mixture. This step helps achieve the desired texture before the pakode even hit the frying pan, setting them up for optimal results.

### Temperature Control for Less Oil Absorption

Perhaps most importantly, Chef Bhadouria emphasizes the critical nature of monitoring oil temperature during frying. Proper temperature control not only ensures crispiness but also significantly reduces oil absorption, addressing health-conscious consumers' concerns about excess grease.
