## Breaking the Red Wine Temperature Rules

Summer entertaining is challenging traditional wine wisdom, with experts increasingly advocating for chilled red wines as the perfect warm-weather solution. According to reports, cooling certain red varietals enhances their flavor profiles, making them ideal alternatives to traditional warm-temperature reds that many wine enthusiasts haven't fully embraced.

Sommeliers and winemakers are highlighting specific varietals that benefit from refrigeration, including pinot noir, gamay, frappato, grenache, and cinsault. The science behind chilling these wines centers on how lower temperatures enhance fruit characteristics and freshness, debunking the long-held myth that red wine must always be served warm.
