# Essential Car Seat Safety: 5 Expert Tips Every Parent Must Know

As parents, nothing matters more than keeping our children safe. When it comes to car travel, having the right car seat is just the beginning – knowing how to use it properly could be the difference between life and death.

![Father buckling toddler daughter into her car seat](https://i2-prod.dailyrecord.co.uk/article36916288.ece/ALTERNATES/s1200/2_Father-buckling-toddler-daughter-in-to-her-car-seat.jpg)

Kat Gemmell, a car seat development specialist at Silver Cross with 16 years of experience in child safety, has seen firsthand how simple mistakes can have devastating consequences. She works directly with design teams, maintains crash-test standards, and ensures every product meets real-world safety needs.

"Simply having a car seat installed isn't enough," Kat explains. "There are crucial steps parents need to follow to truly protect their children while traveling."

Here are her five essential safety tips that every parent should know:

## 1. Know When Your Child Can Graduate from a Car Seat

UK law requires children to use car seats until they're 12 years old or reach 135cm in height, whichever comes first. But determining readiness involves more than just age and height.

Kat recommends this five-step assessment:
- Child must be over 135cm tall
- Back and bottom should sit fully against the seat
- Knees should bend naturally over the seat edge
- Lap belt sits properly at the pelvis
- Shoulder belt crosses the collarbone correctly

"If any of these criteria aren't met, your child will slouch, which could lead to them sliding under the seatbelt during an accident – a dangerous phenomenon called submarining," Kat warns.

## 2. Keep Children Rear-Facing as Long as Possible

While UK law only requires rear-facing seats until 15 months, safety experts strongly recommend keeping children rear-facing much longer.

"Rear-facing seats are significantly safer in car accidents," Kat emphasizes. "In Sweden, where rear-facing seats are extremely popular, many aim to pass the Swedish Plus Test – one of the world's most rigorous safety standards that only rear-facing toddler seats can attempt."

This voluntary test uses high speeds and short braking distances to ensure seats can prevent severe or life-threatening injuries. The demand for Swedish Plus Test-approved seats is growing rapidly among UK parents who prioritize maximum safety.

## 3. Never Buy Second-Hand Car Seats

While purchasing used items can be economical and environmentally friendly, car seats are a critical exception.

"If a car seat has been in an accident, its effectiveness is compromised, putting your child at serious risk," Kat explains. "You also won't receive proper installation instructions, and safety regulations are frequently updated, so older seats may not meet current standards."

The peace of mind that comes with a new car seat, complete with instructions and warranty, is invaluable when it comes to your child's safety.

## 4. Avoid These Two Common Installation Mistakes

**Remove Bulky Coats**: One of the most dangerous mistakes parents make is leaving their child's puffy winter coat on in the car seat.

"Bulky coats prevent you from properly tightening the harness," Kat explains. "In an accident, the coat's stuffing compresses, creating dangerous slack that increases the risk of your child being ejected from their seat."

Instead, buckle your child in first, then place a blanket over them for warmth.

**Ensure Proper Harness Tension**: Many parents don't tighten harnesses sufficiently. Use Kat's simple "pinch test":
- Strap your child in and ensure harnesses aren't twisted
- Pull the adjuster strap to tighten
- Try to pinch the harness webbing at shoulder level
- If you can pinch any fabric, tighten more

"The harness should be snug enough that you cannot pinch any webbing," Kat advises.

## 5. Plan Regular Breaks for Newborns

For families with newborns, extended car travel requires special consideration.

"Newborns should take a break from their car seat every two hours," Kat recommends. "This guideline helps prevent breathing difficulties and allows for necessary feeding, changing, and bonding time."

She also reminds parents that sleep-deprived caregivers need breaks too. "It's unlikely your newborn will happily remain in a car seat for two hours without needing attention, and you need to stay alert while driving."

The Highway Code supports this approach, recommending all drivers stop for at least 15 minutes every two hours.

## Creating a Safer Journey for Your Family

Car seat safety extends far beyond meeting legal requirements – it's about creating multiple layers of protection for your most precious cargo. By following these expert guidelines, you're not just complying with regulations; you're actively safeguarding your child's wellbeing.

Remember, every journey is an opportunity to practice these safety habits. Take the time to properly install and adjust your car seat, and don't hesitate to seek professional help if you're unsure about any aspect of car seat safety.

Your child's safety is worth every extra minute spent ensuring their car seat is properly installed and adjusted. These small actions can make the biggest difference when it matters most.
