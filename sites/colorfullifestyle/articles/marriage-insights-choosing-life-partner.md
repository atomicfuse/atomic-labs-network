# 15 Marriage Insights That Will Transform How You Choose Your Life Partner

Whether you're actively dating or simply thinking about your future, relationship wisdom from those who've walked the path can be incredibly valuable. Recently, married men on Reddit shared their most thoughtful advice about choosing a life partner, and their insights might just change how you approach relationships.

![A smiling couple walks arm in arm along a waterfront at sunset, exuding a relaxed and happy vibe](https://img.buzzfeed.com/buzzfeed-static/static/2026-03/24/16/subbuzz/qeehjVRak.jpg?downsize=700%3A%2A&output-quality=auto&output-format=auto)

Here's what these experienced husbands want you to know:

## 1. Prioritize Communication Above All Else

"Pick someone you can talk to. You're both going to change in all kinds of ways over your lives, physically and mentally, and face all kinds of unexpected hurdles together. If you genuinely like each other's company and are good at talking to each other and figuring things out respectfully when problems arise, you'll be set for whatever curveballs are coming."

This foundational advice reminds us that life is full of surprises, and having a partner who can navigate conversations with you makes all the difference.

![A couple smiling and working together on a laptop at a dining table in a cozy living room](https://img.buzzfeed.com/buzzfeed-static/static/2026-03/24/16/subbuzz/CqECfCZgX.jpg)

## 2. Values Matter More Than Hobbies

"Common values are more important than common interests, and everyone's interests do change when they grow up. If you grow together, you're gonna find or develop a lot of common interests, but incompatible values are very unlikely to ever change."

One married man elaborated: "My wife often tells me we have almost no interests in common, but our values match perfectly. Our goals for our family and each other align beautifully. Having someone who supports you and you want to support in return is extremely important."

## 3. Handle Conflict Like Adults

"Look for someone who communicates honestly and handles conflict without drama. Skills, looks, and fun matter, but how you navigate life's inevitable problems together is what really lasts."

As one husband shared: "Early in our relationship, I phrased something poorly. Instead of getting upset, my wife told me how she interpreted what I said and asked what I meant. That moment showed me her character."

## 4. Choose Kindness Always

"Marry someone you get along with. Someone kind."

Sometimes the most profound advice is also the simplest. Kindness creates a foundation for everything else in a relationship.

## 5. Your Partner Should Be Your Best Friend

"The person you marry should be a friend. I don't know how to explain that any better, but if someone asks who my best friend is, it's always been my wife."

![Two people sitting on a couch, joyfully playing video games with controllers, one wearing a cozy sweater and the other in a casual hoodie](https://img.buzzfeed.com/buzzfeed-static/static/2026-03/24/16/subbuzz/i90IzfMq0.jpg)

This friendship foundation allows couples to enjoy both shared activities and independent hobbies. As one man noted: "I love playing video games while my wife reads next to me. We don't need identical interests to enjoy each other's company."

## 6. Find Someone Who Enhances Your Everyday

"Date the person who makes your average life better."

This beautifully captures the essence of partnership – someone who brings joy to ordinary moments.

## 7. Trust Should Flow Naturally

"Trust should come easily and without conditions or exceptions."

![A smiling couple embraces in a cozy kitchen, radiating warmth and affection](https://img.buzzfeed.com/buzzfeed-static/static/2026-03/24/16/subbuzz/_ulcXGOZY.jpg)

One man shared his painful experience: "I spent over 10 years with someone who couldn't trust. Every day felt like living in a prison, constantly explaining minor things. That's no way to live."

## 8. Start as Friends, Not Just Romantic Partners

"If you go out deciding to 'find a life partner,' you become goal-oriented for the wrong things. The happiest couples I know started as friends first. Many divorces happen because people focused on finding a 'life partner' or 'sex partner' without building genuine friendship."

## 9. Make Financial Decisions Together

"Any big purchase is a group decision. You don't drop $5,000 without speaking to your significant other."

![A smiling couple shares a playful moment in an outdoor market, with the woman playfully holding a snack. They seem happy and engaged with each other](https://img.buzzfeed.com/buzzfeed-static/static/2026-03/24/16/subbuzz/8PyPYQMAz.jpg)

Financial compatibility and shared decision-making are crucial for long-term partnership success.

## 10. Don't Ignore Sexual Compatibility

"Sexual incompatibilities are an absolutely valid reason to break up. If you're not both happy with your intimate life before marriage, you're setting yourself up for frustration and resentment."

This honest advice acknowledges that physical compatibility matters for relationship satisfaction.

## 11. Laugh Together Daily

"Choose someone you can laugh with every day."

![Two people are relaxing together on a couch, with one lying back and the other smiling up at them](https://img.buzzfeed.com/buzzfeed-static/static/2026-03/24/16/subbuzz/NI_H6SLoO.jpg)

Laughter truly is medicine for relationships, helping couples navigate stress and stay connected.

## 12. Establish Healthy Conflict Resolution

"My wife and I have never given each other the silent treatment. We don't use insults or name-calling when arguing. Neither gets jealous if the other goes out alone. My twenties taught me what unhealthy relationships look like."

Learning from past relationship mistakes helps identify red flags and green flags.

## 13. Pay Attention to Where You Meet

"Really try to understand what they actually want. If you meet someone at a party, they probably enjoy partying. If you meet someone at a library, they're probably a reader. Which lifestyle appeals to you?"

![Two people smiling and reading a book together in a library, conveying a sense of shared interest and intimacy](https://img.buzzfeed.com/buzzfeed-static/static/2026-03/24/16/subbuzz/nRjJ0wa54.jpg)

As one man shared: "I met my girlfriend at the library, and she's incredible. My ex loved partying. Know what suits your lifestyle best."

## 14. Never Compromise Your Core Identity

"Don't sacrifice your identity. Early in marriage, I told myself certain things weren't big deals. Over time, those compromises festered and changed who I was. Never settle for less than your version of perfect. Perfect people don't exist, but you can find your perfect match."

## 15. Choose Someone You Admire

"Whoever you marry, you will become more like them. Choose someone you admire."

![Two people in a kitchen, smiling and cooking together, enjoying a joyful moment while preparing a meal on the stove](https://img.buzzfeed.com/buzzfeed-static/static/2026-03/24/16/subbuzz/k1Wc7KeuT.jpg)

This final piece of wisdom beautifully captures how partnerships shape us over time.

## Moving Forward in Love

These insights from married men remind us that successful partnerships are built on friendship, communication, shared values, and mutual respect. Whether you're single, dating, or in a committed relationship, these principles can guide you toward healthier, more fulfilling connections.

Remember, the goal isn't perfection – it's finding someone whose imperfections complement yours, someone who makes your ordinary days extraordinary, and someone whose character you genuinely admire and respect.
