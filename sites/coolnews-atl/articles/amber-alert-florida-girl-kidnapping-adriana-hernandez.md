A desperate search is underway for a 15-year-old girl who authorities believe was kidnapped from her Florida home, prompting an urgent AMBER Alert that has expanded beyond state lines.

![Adriana Hernandez](https://www.usmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Adriana-Hernandez-Police-Issue-AMBER-Alert-for-Girl-Taken-From-Florida-Home.jpg?crop=0px,0px,2000px,1051px&resize=1200,630&quality=86&strip=all)

Adriana Hernandez has been missing from Milton in Florida's Santa Rosa County since Tuesday, April 7th, sparking a multi-state manhunt that has investigators deeply concerned for her safety. The teenager was last seen near the 6400 block of Bruce Lane in Milton, and law enforcement officials fear she may now be in Georgia.

## From Runaway to Kidnapping Case

The case took a dramatic turn when the Santa Rosa County Sheriff's Office, which initially treated Hernandez's disappearance as a runaway situation, discovered compelling evidence suggesting the teenager is in grave danger. The department has since reclassified the investigation as a kidnapping, though specific details about the evidence remain under wraps.

Hernandez is described as a white-Hispanic female standing 5-foot-1 and weighing approximately 130 pounds. She has brown eyes and may have a reddish-brown tint in her hair. Authorities have not released information about her clothing at the time of disappearance or any vehicle she might be traveling in.

## Nationwide Kidnapping Crisis

This alarming case adds to a disturbing pattern of kidnappings reported across the United States this year, highlighting a growing public safety crisis that has law enforcement agencies on high alert.

Just this month, South Carolina authorities arrested 21-year-old Darrius Gilmore on charges of attempting to murder and kidnap two 15-year-old girls. In a harrowing turn of events, one victim managed to escape and seek help, leading to Gilmore's capture. He faces multiple serious charges including attempted murder, kidnapping, firearm possession during violent crimes, and discharging a weapon into a vehicle.

The trend extends beyond individual cases. In West Virginia, Todd Moore, 41, was arrested after allegedly kidnapping a woman who was discovered with shock collar wounds around her neck. The victim told police Moore had held her captive, preventing her from making phone calls or leaving the premises.

Meanwhile, California law enforcement dealt with their own kidnapping case when Alexander Schecter was accused of holding two UCLA students captive inside his vehicle. UCLA Police Department warned there may be additional victims of Schecter, who has no known affiliation with the university.

## Critical Information Needed

Time is of the essence in locating Adriana Hernandez. Authorities are urging anyone with information about her whereabouts to immediately contact:

- FDLE Missing Endangered Persons line: 1-888-356-4774
- Santa Rosa Sheriff's Office: (850) 983-1190
- Or call 911 if you believe you have seen Hernandez

## Public Safety Alert

The string of kidnapping cases across multiple states underscores the importance of community vigilance and immediate reporting of suspicious activities. Law enforcement agencies emphasize that quick action from the public can mean the difference between life and death in these critical situations.

If you know of any young person being exploited or victimized, authorities urge immediate contact with your local FBI field office at 1-800-CALL-FBI. Anonymous tips can also be submitted at tips.fbi.gov.

As the search for Adriana Hernandez intensifies, investigators are working around the clock to bring her home safely. The AMBER Alert system continues to prove its value in mobilizing entire communities to protect our most vulnerable citizens—but success depends on swift public response and cooperation with law enforcement efforts.
