In a dramatic turn of events that has sent shockwaves through the halls of Congress, California Representative Eric Swalwell announced his resignation from office on Monday, April 13, following a cascade of sexual abuse and harassment allegations from multiple women.

![Eric Swalwell](https://www.usmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/GettyImages-2258202352.jpg?crop=303px,39px,1576px,828px&resize=1200,630&quality=86&strip=all)

## A Fall from Grace

The 47-year-old Democrat, who has represented California's 14th congressional district since 2012, issued a statement via social media acknowledging "mistakes in judgement" while maintaining his innocence regarding the most serious allegations.

"I am deeply sorry to my family, staff and constituents for mistakes in judgement I've made in my past," Swalwell wrote in his resignation statement. "I will fight the serious, false allegation made against me. However, I must take responsibility and ownership for the mistakes I did make."

The congressman's decision comes as he faced mounting pressure from both sides of the aisle, with reports of an imminent expulsion vote gaining momentum. Rather than weather the storm, Swalwell chose to step aside, citing his inability to effectively serve his constituents while embroiled in the scandal.

"Expelling anyone in Congress without due process, within days of an allegation being made, is wrong," he continued. "But it's also wrong for my constituents to have me distracted from my duties. Therefore I plan to resign my seat in Congress."

## The Allegations Unfold

The controversy erupted in April when a former staffer came forward with explosive allegations against Swalwell. The woman, who was just 21 when hired to work at his Castro Valley district office in 2019, alleged to the San Francisco Chronicle that the congressman pursued her romantically in deeply inappropriate ways.

![Eric Swalwell at congressional hearing](https://www.usmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Eric-Swalwell-GettyImages-2257621137.jpg?w=1000&quality=86&strip=all)

According to her account, Swalwell sent explicit images via Snapchat and solicited nude photographs in return. Most seriously, she accused the congressman of rape – an allegation that has triggered a Manhattan District Attorney's investigation.

Following the initial report, additional accusers emerged from the shadows. Ally Sammarco, who chose to go public with her name, alleged that Swalwell offered to "share her resume" with other congressional offices before sending her "very inappropriate" Snapchat messages. Other women have come forward with similar claims but have requested anonymity due to fears of retribution.

## Vehement Denials and Personal Apologies

Swalwell has categorically denied the criminal allegations, framing them as politically motivated attacks timed to coincide with his gubernatorial ambitions.

"These allegations are false and come on the eve of an election against the frontrunner for governor," he stated. "For nearly 20 years, I have served the public — as a prosecutor and a congressman and have always protected women."

However, the congressman did issue a public apology to his wife, Brittany Watts, whom he married in 2016, acknowledging unspecified "mistakes in judgment."

"I do not suggest to you in any way that I am perfect or that I'm a saint. I have certainly made mistakes in judgment in my past," Swalwell said in an April social media video. "Those mistakes are between me and my wife. And to her, I apologize deeply for putting her in this position."

## Staff Abandonment

Perhaps most damaging to Swalwell's position was a statement released by his own staff members, who publicly distanced themselves from their boss and expressed support for the accusers.

"As leaders of teams working for Eric Swalwell, we're horrified by the recent reporting in the San Francisco Chronicle and by CNN," the staff statement read. "We stand with our former colleague, and the other women who have come forward. We believe you should stand with them, too. The behavior detailed in these reports is abhorrent, beneath the dignity of those serving in public office and betrays the trust of all Californians."

## Political Career in Ruins

Swalwell's resignation marks the end of what was once a promising political trajectory. First elected to Congress in 2012, he made an unsuccessful bid for the presidency in 2020, dropping out due to low polling numbers. More recently, he had emerged as a frontrunner in California's gubernatorial race before the scandal erupted.

The congressman was forced to suspend his gubernatorial campaign as the allegations mounted, stating: "I am suspending my campaign for Governor. To my family, staff, friends, and supporters, I am deeply sorry for mistakes in judgment I've made in my past. I will fight the serious, false allegations that have been made — but that's my fight, not a campaign's."

## The Broader Implications

Swalwell's downfall represents another chapter in the ongoing reckoning with sexual misconduct in American politics. His resignation, while avoiding the spectacle of an expulsion vote, leaves his constituents without representation and raises questions about the vetting processes for political candidates.

As investigations continue and legal proceedings potentially loom, the full scope of the allegations against Swalwell may yet come to light. What remains certain is that a political career that once showed promise on the national stage has come to an abrupt and ignominious end.

The congressman indicated he would work with his remaining staff to ensure continuity of services for his district constituents during the transition period, though the timeline for a special election to fill his seat remains unclear.
