A harrowing case of alleged child abuse and cover-up has emerged from South Florida, where two young parents now face serious charges in connection with their newborn son's death. The disturbing details reveal a calculated attempt to conceal what authorities believe was a preventable tragedy.

![Crystal Garcia and Anfernee Watts mugshots](https://www.usmagazine.com/wp-content/uploads/2026/04/Florida-Couple-Accused-of-Staging-Infant-Death-Crystal-Garcia-and-Anfernee-Watts-Mugshot.jpg?crop=0px,43px,2000px,1051px&resize=1200,630&quality=86&strip=all)

Crystal Garcia, 21, and Anfernee Watts, 25, were arrested Wednesday, April 10, following an extensive investigation into the death of their 3-week-old son in Hollywood, Florida, last August. The couple now faces a litany of serious charges that paint a devastating picture of neglect and deception.

## The Charges and Investigation

Both parents have been charged with aggravated child manslaughter, child neglect involving bodily harm, tampering with or fabricating physical evidence, failure to report a death to a medical examiner, and providing false information to law enforcement. Court records indicate the infant died from suffocation after being left "restrained" and alone in a bathtub.

According to arrest affidavits, the tragedy unfolded on August 1, 2025, when the baby was found "unresponsive" in a playpen at their Hollywood residence. First responders who arrived at the scene declared the newborn dead, triggering an investigation that would eventually unravel the parents' alleged cover-up.

## A Web of Lies Begins to Unravel

The investigation revealed a disturbing pattern of deception as Garcia and Watts allegedly provided "several differing stories" about their son's death. Initially, Watts told authorities he had left the home for a job interview, claiming Garcia had fed the baby, changed his diaper, and placed him in the playpen before the infant's death.

However, as investigators pressed for the truth, Garcia's story began to change dramatically. Eventually, she admitted to staging the scene to make it appear as though her son had died peacefully in his sleep.

## The Horrifying Truth Emerges

The actual circumstances, as revealed through Garcia's eventual confession, paint a chilling picture of neglect. According to the affidavit, Garcia described putting her crying newborn in a car seat and placing him in a bathtub, where he would remain alone and distressed.

In what investigators describe as an intentional act of restraint, Garcia "placed the baby's gray pacifier in his mouth, wrapped him in the blanket tightly, securing the pacifier in his mouth to prevent him from spitting it out, and strapped him into the car seat." She then placed the car seat containing her son inside the bathtub.

The affidavit reveals that Garcia played loud lullaby music in an apparent attempt to drown out the infant's cries. Despite being able to hear her baby crying "very faintly" over the music, she left him alone and went to sleep.

## A Mother's Devastating Discovery

When Garcia awoke the following morning, she first spent approximately 30 minutes speaking with her mother before checking on her son. Upon discovering the infant, she found him "very pale" and immediately "knew it was dead," according to the court documents.

Investigators determined that both Garcia and Watts collaborated in staging the crime scene, moving their deceased son from the bathroom to the playpen in an attempt to create the illusion of a natural death during sleep.

## Official Determination

Through Garcia's sworn admission, corroborated by physical evidence, digital evidence, and witness statements, authorities concluded that she had "intentionally restrained the baby." The medical examiner's findings confirmed the infant died as a result of negligence and suffocation.

This case represents yet another tragic example of child abuse in Florida, highlighting the vulnerability of infants who depend entirely on their caregivers for protection and care. The charges against Garcia and Watts carry significant potential penalties, reflecting the severity of their alleged actions.

As this case moves through the legal system, it serves as a stark reminder of the devastating consequences that can result from neglect and the lengths some individuals will go to conceal their actions. The legal proceedings will ultimately determine the fate of these young parents who now face the full weight of the justice system for their alleged role in their infant son's preventable death.
