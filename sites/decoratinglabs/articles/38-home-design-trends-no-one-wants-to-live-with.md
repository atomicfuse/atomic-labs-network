### TL;DR
While modern home designs often look stunning in photographs, many of them are impractical for everyday living. Here are 38 home design trends to avoid if you want a comfortable and functional space.

---

When it comes to home design, striking the perfect balance between aesthetics and functionality can feel like a daunting challenge. Instagram might showcase beautiful modern homes, but Reddit users have shared a list of eye-opening trends that are garnering serious backlash. From impractical layouts to uncomfortable choices, these design trends may seem appealing at first glance, but they often fall short in real-life application. Let’s delve into the 38 controversial home design trends that homeowners wish would just disappear. 

### 1. Removing Stair Banisters
Many modern designs advocate for the minimalist look, leading to the removal of stair banisters. This trend not only looks precarious but also poses a safety hazard, especially for families with kids.

### 2. Open Concept Bathrooms
While open concept living spaces are celebrated, the bathroom is one area where privacy is key. Open bathrooms can be uncomfortable, making daily routines feel exposed and awkward.

### 3. Too Many Pot Lights
Consideration of lighting is crucial in any home, but too many pot lights can be overkill. Instead of achieving a cozy atmosphere, the result is often a stark, hospital-like vibe.

### 4. An Overabundance of Bathrooms
More bathrooms might seem appealing, but they can lead to excessive maintenance and an unbalanced layout, making the home feel like a maze.

### 5. Agreeable Gray Everywhere
This neutral shade was once considered versatile, but it has now become ubiquitous, leading to homes that lack personality and warmth.

### 6. Lack of Storage Space
In modern home designs, ample storage seems to take a backseat. This trend results in clutter and chaos, making it difficult for homeowners to keep their space organized.

### 7. No Door Between Primary Bedroom and Bathroom
Eliminating the door between your bedroom and bathroom sounds appealing for an open feel. However, it can lead to a lack of privacy and noise disturbances.

### 8. Glass Railings on Stairs or Decks
Although glass railings can look sleek, they require constant cleaning and may not be safe for homes with children or pets.

### 9. Built-in Appliances Integrated Into Walls
While seamless designs are trending, wall-integrated appliances can limit flexibility in the kitchen and make repairs a hassle.

### 10. Beautiful Front Exteriors with Cheap Vinyl Backs
Homeowners investing in attractive exteriors often regret when the back looks cheap and uninviting, detracting from the overall appeal of the property.

### 11. Modern Farmhouse Style
The romanticized version of modern farmhouse décor is often impractical, combining too many contrasting elements that just don’t work in real life.

### 12. Floor-to-Ceiling Clear Glass Showers
Yes, they look stunning, but clear glass showers require constant upkeep and can feel more like an exhibit than a place of relaxation.

### 13. Microwave ‘Vent Hoods’
Vent hoods that are merely decorative can create more problems than they solve. A kitchen without proper ventilation is not only unappealing but can also lead to cooking mishaps.

### 14. Narrow Rectangle Kitchens
Designers may think a narrow kitchen is an efficient use of space, but they often create a cramped cooking environment that’s impractical for culinary adventures.

### 15. Humongous Kitchen Islands
While large kitchen islands can serve as a focal point, they can also dominate the space and disrupt the flow in smaller kitchens.

### 16. Huge Front-Facing Windows
Oversized windows may bring in plenty of natural light, but they can also cause privacy concerns and excess heat during summer months.

### 17. Counter-Depth Fridges
These fridges are designed to be flush with the cabinetry, but they often lose functionality, with less space for groceries and storage.

### 18. Badly Designed Sinks
Style over substance leads to sinks that are hard to clean or impractical. Choose options that are both aesthetically pleasing and functional.

### 19. Overdone Gray-and-White Minimalism
This minimalist style may seem chic, but too much gray and white can create a bland, hospital-like environment in your home.

### 20. Open Shelving Instead of Cabinetry
Open shelving offers visual appeal but can lead to clutter, where every dish is on display and dust easily accumulates.

### 21. No Broom Closets
In the quest for streamlined spaces, many new builds ignore the need for broom closets, leading to storage chaos instead of convenience.

### 22. TVs Mounted Over Fireplaces
Mounting TVs over fireplaces is not as practical as it sounds. It can make viewing uncomfortable and leads to issues with heat damage.

### 23. Too-Small Two-Car Garages
Designers often underestimate the size needed for a comfortable two-car garage, leading to frustration for homeowners.

### 24. Painting Over Brick
Painting over brick can destroy the natural charm of a home’s exterior and can lead to peeling and maintenance headaches later on.

### 25. Tiny Laundry Rooms, Pantries, and Closets
Minimizing spaces like laundry rooms is a common trend, but it often backfires, leading to cramped and cluttered areas that nobody enjoys.

### What to Keep in Mind
As trends come and go, the most important aspect of home design lies in creating a space that feels lived in and comfortable. While it’s tempting to chase after the latest Instagram aesthetic, remember that a home should be functional first and foremost. For every trendy feature that captures your eye, consider how it will truly serve you in the long run.

In the current ‘anti-trend’ mood, practicality is reigning supreme. By avoiding these 38 controversial trends, you can invest in a home that looks great and works for you, both now and in the future.
