## TL;DR
Not loving your rental? With these budget-friendly upgrades, you can transform your space without breaking the bank or risking your deposit. From stylish storage to bold art, small changes can make a big impact!

## Introduction
Let’s face it: not all apartments are cute. They often come with outdated features and bland color schemes that do little to inspire. But don’t despair! If you're a renter on a budget, there’s a whole world of affordable, reversible design hacks waiting for you. Borrowing inspiration from the clever tips of interior designer Tommy Landen Huerter, we’re about to dive into brilliant apartment upgrades that will make your rental feel stylish, intentional, and, best of all, like *home*.

### 1. Layer Unexpected Decor in the Kitchen
The kitchen is often the heart of the home, but if it feels lackluster, it’s time to make some changes. Try layering unexpected decor items like a vibrant fruit bowl, a colorful cutting board, or chic cookbooks displayed on open shelving. Adding a few plants can also breathe life into a dull space. Opt for easy-care options like succulents or snake plants to keep your kitchen looking fresh and lively without too much effort!

### 2. Swap Kitchen Cabinet Hardware
One of the simplest and most effective upgrades you can make is to swap out your kitchen cabinet hardware. This small change can dramatically elevate the style of your kitchen. Choose unique knobs or handles that reflect your personal style—think vintage brass for a classic look or sleek black for a modern touch. You can typically find these at home improvement stores or online retailers for a great price.

### 3. Add a Peel-and-Stick Backsplash
If your kitchen walls are uninspiring, peel-and-stick backsplashes can be a game-changer. These affordable materials come in a variety of styles, from classic subway tiles to colorful mosaics, and they’re super easy to apply yourself! They peel right off when it’s time to move, leaving your landlord none the wiser. A bold backsplash can add personality and create a focal point in your kitchen.

### 4. Put a Rug Over Carpeting
Wall-to-wall carpet can often feel dated or cumbersome. Combat this by layering a stylish area rug over existing carpeting. Not only does it offer a design element, but it can also create a cozier atmosphere. Choose a rug that complements your decor scheme and makes the area feel more intentional. Plus, it’s a quick way to add warmth and comfort underfoot!

### 5. Switch Out Ceiling Light Fixtures
The lighting in many rentals is basic at best. If you're allowed, swapping out a standard ceiling light fixture for something more stylish can change the entire ambiance of a room. Look for fixtures that match your aesthetic—maybe a pendant light over a dining area or a chic chandelier for your living room. Just remember to keep the original fixture safe and in case your landlord wants it back!

### 6. Layer Lighting for Depth
Let’s talk lighting… and lots of it! Layering different types of lighting—think lamps, uplights, and under-cabinet lights—can create depth and make your rental feel warm and inviting. A combination of ambient, task, and accent lighting allows you to set the mood based on the time of day and activity. Choose lamps with unique designs to expose your personality even further!

### 7. Upgrade Small Bathroom Details
The bathroom is often neglected in rental upgrades. You can easily inject personality with simple updates like new towel racks, a stylish shower curtain, or fresh bath mats. Consider replacing the faucet fixtures for a quick refresh that feels both modern and chic. These small tweaks can make your bathroom feel more like a spa retreat.

### 8. Hang Bold Art in the Bathroom
Don’t underestimate the power of art—even in the bathroom! Hang a bright piece of art or a mirror with a unique frame to make the space more visually engaging. This inexpensive improvement can instantly elevate the room’s vibe and make you feel more at home every time you step in.

### 9. Use Stylish Storage Solutions
Clutter can easily make a space feel cramped and chaotic, especially in smaller rentals. Upgrade your bathroom by investing in stylish storage solutions—think elegant baskets, floating shelves, or chic bins that hide the clutter while looking great. By keeping your space organized, your bathroom will feel more like a serene retreat.

## Conclusion
With a little creativity and some budget-friendly choices, making your rental feel like a true home is totally achievable. These updates are not only easy but also renter-friendly, meaning you can revamp your space without losing your security deposit. So why wait? Start implementing these stylish upgrades today and transform your rental into the cozy oasis you've always dreamed of!
