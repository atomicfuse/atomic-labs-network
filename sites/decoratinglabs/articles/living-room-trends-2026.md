TL;DR: Designers are moving away from sleek, sterile living rooms in favor of warm, functional spaces. Discover which trends to ditch for 2026 and how to refresh your home decor!

## What’s Out for 2026? Trends You Can Ditch

As we look toward the future of home design in 2026, it seems that the polished and perfectly curated aesthetic is already feeling dated. Instead, designers are championing inviting, lived-in environments that reflect personal style rather than industry trends. Here are some living room trends to say goodbye to, and practical alternatives to consider!

### 1. The Neutral RH Aesthetic

**Why It’s Out:** The all-beige, neutral color palettes that have dominated living rooms are starting to cloy. Many designers feel that this look has become overly safe and lacks personality.

**What to Do Instead:** Embrace color! Consider adding warm tones like terracotta, muted greens, or vibrant blues. Accent walls or colorful decor can transform your space without overwhelming it. Mixing and matching colorful cushions and throws is an easy way to start infusing personality into your living room.

### 2. Hidden TVs

**Why It’s Out:** The trend of hiding televisions behind cabinetry or artwork might seem chic, but it ultimately undermines functionality. Designers argue that it can make the room feel uninviting and overly designed.

**What to Do Instead:** Designers recommend showcasing your TV as a practical component of your living space. Opt for a creative entertainment unit that combines style with functionality, and arrange seating to foster a cozy viewing experience. Consider surrounding your TV with playful elements that reflect your interests — think art or fun lighting!

### 3. An Industrial Influence

**Why It’s Out:** While industrial elements brought a modern edge to many homes, this trend is becoming stale, with raw materials and stark lines leading to impersonal environments.

**What to Do Instead:** Swap out sharp, industrial decor for softer, more organic materials. Incorporate natural fibers, wooden accents, and organic shapes to create a warmer, more inviting living room. A few well-chosen plants can also help breathe life into the space!

### 4. Rounded Bouclé Chairs and Sofas

**Why It’s Out:** Though bouclé pieces have made a splash in living room design, there are mixed opinions on their practicality and comfort.

**What to Do Instead:** Instead of committing to an entire bouclé set, consider adding a few curated statement pieces along with more flexible seating options. Mix different textures and styles to create balance. Perhaps pair a soft bouclé chair with sleek leather or linen upholstery for an interesting dynamic!

### 5. Gallery Wall Grids

**Why It’s Out:** The meticulous, symmetrical gallery wall that has been a staple for years is losing its luster. Instead of feeling homey, they can come off as sterile and overdone.

**What to Do Instead:** Opt for a more casual, eclectic display for your walls! Combine different frame sizes and styles for a collected look that tells your story. Showcase personal photographs, unique art pieces, and even DIY creations to cultivate a space rich with memories and character.

### 6. Matching Wood Tones

**Why It’s Out:** Matching wood tones may have seemed like an easy choice, but it often leads to a monotonous visual effect and lacks the depth needed in today's interiors.

**What to Do Instead:** Experiment with mixing wood tones! Combining different shades and textures can lend your space an effortless warmth and authenticity. Consider pairing dark woods with lighter pieces for contrast, and don't shy away from mixing in metal accents for a layered look.

## Why It Matters Now

As we rapidly approach 2026, these trends remind us that home design isn’t just about adhering to trends; it’s about creating spaces that truly reflect who we are. Rather than striving for editorial perfection, why not focus on making your home comfortable, colorful, and functional?

### Conclusion

With a few thoughtful adjustments, you can easily update your living space to stay current with 2026’s design ethos. Let each piece and decor choice be a conversation starter, a reflection of your personality, and most importantly, a space where you feel at home. So grab those paint swatches, shop for some vibrant decor, and let your creativity flow!
