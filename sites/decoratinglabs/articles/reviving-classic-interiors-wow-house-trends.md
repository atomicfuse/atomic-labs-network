## The Soul of Modern Craftsmanship

If you've been feeling overwhelmed by the fast pace of current design trends, you're not alone. Many homeowners crave a sense of personal comfort and authenticity in their spaces. At London's latest design showcase, the WOW!house, the focus was on timeless craftsmanship and historic techniques reimagined for today's homes. Let's dive into five design trends you can easily incorporate into your own space![1]  

## 1. The Verdigris Effect: Green Your Space

Verdigris, that striking green patina seen on aged copper, is making waves in the interior design world. This unique finish adds a rich texture and depth that breathes life into home decor. You can easily introduce the verdigris effect into your home by:

- **Accessorizing**: Look for copper planters, decorative bowls, or artwork finished with verdigris. These small pieces create a strong visual impact without overwhelming your space.
- **Paint Techniques**: If you're feeling particularly adventurous, why not try out a DIY paint technique to achieve a verdigris look on an accent wall or furniture? This works beautifully with wooden furniture, adding a historical elegance.

## 2. Damask Is Back: Patterns with a Purpose

Yes, you heard it right! Damask is enjoying a stylish resurgence. This classic pattern, often associated with luxury, is a warm way to add character to your interiors. Here’s how to incorporate it into your home:

- **Textiles**: Whether through wallpaper, upholstery, or curtains, damask can add a touch of sophistication to any room. Opt for softer colors to keep the look contemporary.
- **Mix and Match**: Pair damask with solid colors or even other patterns for a delightful layered look. This blend keeps the vibe fresh and doesn’t make the space feel too formal.

## 3. Architectural Tailoring: Details Matter

Gone are the days when minimalism ruled supreme. Today, the trend leans toward generously tailored details that resemble fine clothing. Here’s how to bring this elegance into your home:

- **Crown Molding and Trim**: Invest in decorative trim for ceilings and windows. This adds an instant touch of luxury without major renovations.
- **Fabric-Wrapped Details**: Think upholstered walls or custom drapes that provide warmth and texture, mimicking the feel of a well-tailored suit. The key is to layer fabrics that complement your existing decor while enhancing coziness.

## 4. Old-World Techniques Reimagined

Design is making a poignant return to its roots. Techniques like plaster finish, trompe-l'œil, and artisanal tile work are all witnessing a renaissance. Here’s how to incorporate these old-world charms into modern spaces:

- **Handcrafted Elements**: Seek out artisanal pieces that tell a story—think handmade pottery, unique light fixtures, or custom cabinetry.
- **DIY Projects**: You can easily attempt a plaster finish on a feature wall or try your hand at faux painting to emulate historical techniques.

## 5. Layered Comfort: The Maximalist Influence

The current movement towards maximalism invites you to be bolder with color, pattern, and art. It’s about creating a space that feels layered, personal, and lived in. Here’s how to achieve it:

- **Mix Textures and Colors**: Combine various textures—think velvet cushions with woven throws and polished wood. The contrast keeps the room dynamic.
- **Personal Touches**: Fill your walls with art, photos, and treasured items that reflect your journey. A gallery wall layered with personal memories can anchor a room beautifully.
