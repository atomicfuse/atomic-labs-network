## Lighting Studio Ventures Into New Territory

In Common With, the cult-favorite lighting design studio founded by Nick Ozemba and Felicia Hung, is making waves in the home design world with its expansion beyond lighting. The studio has launched its new Lido collection, marking a significant step into the homewares market through a strategic collaboration with Venice-based glassware company Laguna~B.

According to reports, this move represents more than just product diversification—it signals the studio's evolution from a lighting-only brand into a full lifestyle design studio. The collaboration combines traditional Murano glassmaking techniques with contemporary design aesthetics, creating pieces that bridge heritage craftsmanship with modern sensibilities.

## The Art of Murano Meets Modern Design

The Lido collection showcases the studio's commitment to quality craftsmanship through its use of traditional Murano glassmaking techniques. Reports indicate the collection incorporates specialized methods including goti de fornasa and murrine techniques, bringing centuries-old artisanal skills into contemporary lighting and glassware design.

This approach aligns with In Common With's philosophy of building what they describe as a "world" rather than a single-category brand. The collaboration with Laguna~B demonstrates how the studio uses partnerships and modular design thinking to scale their offerings without compromising on craft quality.

## Strategic Retail and Market Positioning

The expansion comes at a time when the studio has been testing new concepts through their Tribeca concept store, Quarters. According to reports, this experiential retail space serves as both a showcase for product expansion and a testing ground for new collections. The store represents the studio's commitment to creating immersive brand experiences that go beyond traditional retail.
