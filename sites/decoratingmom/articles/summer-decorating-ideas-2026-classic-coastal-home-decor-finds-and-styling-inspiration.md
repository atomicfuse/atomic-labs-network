Summer decorating in 2026 is all about creating a home that feels fresh, welcoming, and easy to live in. Based on Lauren Midgley's summer decorating video, this seasonal refresh brings together traditional and classic home design with Nancy Meyers-inspired charm, cottage softness, and coastal accents. The result is a summer look that feels timeless rather than trendy, with pieces that can be styled throughout the season in a natural and relaxed way.

This summer decorating approach begins with a shopping trip to HomeGoods and Homesense, followed by a decor haul and a decorate-with-me styling session at home. The video highlights the fun of searching for summer designer finds, then using those items to create a warm, polished, and inviting seasonal look. Instead of completely changing a room, the focus is on thoughtful decorative touches, soft textures, pretty florals, and classic color inspiration.

## Classic Summer Decorating with a Timeless Feel

A traditional and classic decorating style is a strong foundation for summer decor because it does not rely on overly specific trends. Instead, it uses familiar, well-loved design elements that feel comfortable year after year. In this summer home decorating inspiration, the style is described as traditional and classic, with cottage and coastal elements layered in for the warmer months.

That combination works especially well for summer because it balances structure with softness. Traditional pieces can ground a room, while coastal and cottage details make the space feel lighter and more seasonal. Rather than using bold seasonal themes, the look can be built through subtle colors, natural-looking florals, patterned textiles, and decor that feels collected over time.

## Nancy Meyers-Inspired Summer Home Style

Nancy Meyers-inspired interiors are often associated with cozy, elegant, lived-in homes that feel both styled and inviting. In the context of summer decorating, that inspiration translates beautifully into a home that feels relaxed but still intentional. It is less about creating a showroom look and more about making everyday spaces feel thoughtful, warm, and seasonal.

This kind of summer style can be achieved through classic accents, comfortable textures, and decor that feels personal. A pillow cover, a planter, or a floral stem can shift the mood of a space without making it feel overly decorated. The goal is to create rooms that feel like they belong to summer: bright, calm, comfortable, and easy to enjoy.

## Shopping for Summer Decor at HomeGoods and Homesense

The shopping portion of the video begins at HomeGoods and Homesense, two stores known for offering a rotating selection of home decor, seasonal pieces, and designer-inspired finds. A summer shop-with-me is useful because it shows how seasonal decorating often starts with browsing, comparing textures, and looking for pieces that fit an existing home style.

When shopping for summer decor, it helps to think about the mood you want your home to have. For a traditional, cottage, and coastal mix, pieces with soft color, classic patterns, and easy-to-style shapes are especially useful. Items that can be moved from one room to another are also practical because they allow you to refresh different areas without needing a full redesign.

The video highlights a blue and white striped pillow cover, which fits naturally into a classic coastal summer palette. Blue and white is a timeless combination, and stripes bring in a crisp, tailored feeling that still feels relaxed enough for summer. This type of piece can work well with traditional decor because it adds seasonal freshness without overpowering the room.

## Florals and Greenery for a Summer Refresh

Flowers and greenery are an easy way to bring summer into the home. The video features flowers in an outdoor planter and green hydrangeas, both of which support a soft seasonal look. Florals can make a space feel brighter and more finished, whether they are used outdoors, on a table, or as part of a styled vignette.

Green hydrangeas are especially versatile for this decorating style because they feel classic rather than overly themed. Their color works well with cottage and coastal elements, and they can blend into a neutral or traditional home without demanding too much attention. Used thoughtfully, greenery can create a fresh summer feeling while still keeping the overall look calm and cohesive.

Outdoor planters are another simple way to extend seasonal decorating beyond the inside of the home. Even a small update to an outdoor area can make the whole home feel more ready for summer. Flowers in a planter can create a welcoming first impression and connect the exterior of the home with the lighter, brighter styling happening indoors.

## Styling a Summer Decor Haul at Home

After shopping, the decor haul and styling portion of the video focuses on placing the selected items around the home. This is where summer decorating becomes more personal. A haul is not just about what was purchased; it is about how each piece works with the home's existing style and how it helps create a seasonal atmosphere.

One helpful styling idea is to layer new summer pieces into areas that already feel established. A pillow cover can refresh existing seating, florals can update a tabletop or entryway, and outdoor planter flowers can add color before guests even walk inside. These small changes allow a home to feel newly styled for summer without losing its familiar character.

Because the style combines traditional, classic, cottage, and coastal inspiration, balance is important. Too many coastal elements can make a room feel themed, while too many formal pieces can make summer decor feel heavy. Mixing soft florals, crisp stripes, and classic accents helps create a look that feels polished but not stiff.

## Simple Takeaways for Summer Decorating 2026

The biggest takeaway from this summer decorating inspiration is that seasonal style does not have to be complicated. A few carefully chosen pieces from a HomeGoods and Homesense shopping trip can help shift the feeling of a home for summer. By focusing on classic details, coastal touches, cottage charm, and inviting Nancy Meyers-style warmth, the finished look can feel both fresh and timeless.

If you are decorating for summer in 2026, start with the pieces that make your home feel lighter and more welcoming. Look for textures, colors, florals, and accents that fit your existing style, then layer them into your rooms with intention. Whether you are styling green hydrangeas, adding a blue and white striped pillow cover, or refreshing an outdoor planter, summer decorating can be simple, beautiful, and personal.
