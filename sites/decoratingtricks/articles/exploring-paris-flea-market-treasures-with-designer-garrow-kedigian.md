The allure of Paris's flea markets is undeniable, offering a treasure trove of unique finds and timeless pieces. In this episode of The Paris Project, acclaimed New York-based interior designer Garrow Kedigian invites us to join him on an inspiring journey through Paul Bert Serpette, the legendary flea market located just outside of Paris. This bustling market is renowned for its eclectic mix of antiques, art, and vintage decor, making it the perfect hunting ground for Garrow's chic new Paris apartment.

During his visit, Garrow navigates through the market's vibrant alleys, sharing insights on his favorite vendors and uncovering remarkable pieces such as Murano glass chandeliers and gilded mirrors. These exquisite finds are set to enhance the elegant aesthetics of his new living space. Each piece tells a story, adding character and depth to the interiors Garrow is meticulously curating for his Parisian abode.

Returning to the apartment, Garrow provides an exciting update on the renovation progress. The transformation is well underway, with each new addition bringing the space closer to his vision of modern elegance intertwined with classic Parisian charm. This behind-the-scenes look reveals not only the beauty of the market treasures but also the thoughtful design process involved in crafting a harmonious home environment.

Paul Bert Serpette stands as a beacon for design enthusiasts, offering an endless array of inspiration. For Garrow Kedigian, these market excursions are not merely shopping trips but opportunities to connect with the past and integrate its beauty into contemporary living. As the renovation unfolds, viewers are treated to a glimpse of Garrow's creative process, demonstrating how elements from diverse eras and styles can coexist beautifully.

Garrow's journey through the Paris flea market serves as a testament to the magic of thoughtful design, where each chosen piece is a cherished part of a larger story.
