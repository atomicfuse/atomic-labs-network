Tineke Triggs, a renowned interior designer, shares her expertise on transforming homes into curated spaces that reflect personal style while ensuring functionality. Her approach emphasizes the importance of personalizing interiors by blending aesthetics with practical elements, creating environments that are both beautiful and livable.

Understanding Tineke's philosophy begins with recognizing the value of curated spaces. A curated home is one where every element is thoughtfully selected and positioned to tell a story about the people who live there. This design method goes beyond mere decoration, focusing on the interplay of colors, textures, and forms to create a cohesive and inviting atmosphere.

## Personalization: The Heart of Curated Design

Tineke Triggs advocates for personalization as the cornerstone of curated design. She believes that a home should be a reflection of the inhabitants' personalities, experiences, and aspirations. To achieve this, she encourages homeowners to incorporate personal artifacts, family heirlooms, and bespoke pieces into the design. This not only adds depth to the decor but also ensures that the space feels authentically theirs.

Incorporating personal items doesn't mean cluttering the space. Tineke advises selecting a few meaningful pieces that can act as focal points in the room. These items should complement the overall design theme while adding a personal touch that sets the space apart.

### Balancing Aesthetics and Functionality

One of the key challenges in interior design is balancing beauty with practicality. Tineke Triggs excels in this aspect by ensuring that every piece of furniture and accessory serves a dual purpose. For instance, a stylish coffee table might also offer storage, or a decorative ladder could double as a towel rack in the bathroom.

Functionality is crucial, especially in homes with limited space. By selecting multifunctional furniture and creatively utilizing every inch of space, Tineke ensures that her designs are not only visually appealing but also cater to the practical needs of everyday life.

## Color and Texture: The Building Blocks of Design

Color and texture play pivotal roles in Tineke's design strategy. She uses them to set the mood and tone of a space. Her approach involves selecting a base color palette that reflects the homeowner's personality and then layering textures to add depth and interest. This might include mixing fabrics like velvet with rougher materials such as jute or incorporating metallic finishes to add a touch of glamour.

Tineke advises homeowners to be bold with color choices, suggesting that even a small splash of a vibrant hue can uplift a space. However, she also stresses the importance of balance, ensuring that the overall color scheme remains harmonious and not overwhelming.

### The Role of Art in Curated Interiors

Art is another element Tineke Triggs often incorporates to enhance a room's character. She views art not just as decoration but as a conversation starter and an expression of individuality. Whether it's a large statement piece or a collection of smaller works, art can significantly impact the ambiance of a room.

Choosing the right art involves considering the scale, style, and color palette of the piece in relation to the rest of the room. Tineke encourages homeowners to choose pieces that resonate personally with them, thereby adding an emotional layer to the decor.

## Creating a Cohesive Flow

Achieving a cohesive flow throughout the home is essential for a curated look. Tineke Triggs accomplishes this by maintaining a consistent design language across different rooms. This doesn't mean each room must look identical; instead, there should be elements that tie the spaces together, such as a recurring color, pattern, or material.

She also emphasizes the importance of considering the home's architecture when planning the interior design. By aligning the decor with the architectural features, Tineke ensures that the design feels integrated and seamless.

### The Impact of Lighting

Lighting is a crucial aspect of interior design that Tineke Triggs uses to enhance the mood and functionality of a space. She incorporates a mix of ambient, task, and accent lighting to create layers of light that can be adjusted according to need. This approach not only makes a space more versatile but also highlights key design elements.

Strategically placed lighting can transform a room, drawing attention to artwork, highlighting architectural features, or simply creating a warm and inviting atmosphere.

In conclusion, Tineke Triggs' approach to curated design is about more than just aesthetics. It's about creating spaces that tell a story, reflect personal style, and enhance the quality of life. By focusing on personalization, functionality, and cohesiveness, she helps homeowners transform their spaces into truly unique environments.
