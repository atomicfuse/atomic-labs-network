When it comes to home design, the art of transformation is a delicate dance between respecting the past and embracing the future. This was the balancing act that interior designer Tanya Mallavarapu faced when she took on the task of renovating her in-laws' 30-year-old family residence. The result is a visually stunning art haven that seamlessly blends tradition and modernity.

Previously, the home was a testament to the popular design aesthetic of its time, laden with dark wood and granite. However, the need for a brighter, more open space led to a design overhaul. Tanya took the challenge head-on, promising to preserve the essence of the home while introducing a fresh, contemporary outlook.

## Transitioning from Old to New

As the renovation got underway, the first order of business was to let in more natural light. To achieve this, the dark, heavy wooden doors were replaced with lighter, glass-panelled ones, and the granite flooring was swapped out for lighter marble. The walls were painted in a soothing palette of whites and ivories, providing a blank canvas for the home's true stars – the art pieces.

## Creating an Art Haven

Art taking center stage was an essential aspect of the redesign. Interestingly, the home's original architect was an artist himself, and his sketches were prominently displayed. Tanya decided to expand on this artistic heritage, carefully choosing pieces that would add character and depth to the space. From large, vibrant paintings to delicate, intricate sculptures, each piece was thoughtfully selected and positioned to enhance the home's aesthetics.

## The Power of Personal Touches

While the home now boasts a modern, minimalistic design, it was crucial to ensure it retained its warmth and familial essence. This was achieved through the inclusion of personal touches. Family photos were adorned in unique frames, adding an intimate feel to the space. Custom-made furniture, tailored to the family's needs, added functionality while ensuring comfort. The result is a home that beautifully marries the past and present while celebrating the power of art.

The transformation of this 30-year-old house into a contemporary art haven is a testament to Tanya's vision and skill. She has masterfully navigated the fine line between respecting tradition and embracing modernity. The result is a home that is not only visually stunning but also tells a poignant tale of family, heritage, and the transformative power of design.
