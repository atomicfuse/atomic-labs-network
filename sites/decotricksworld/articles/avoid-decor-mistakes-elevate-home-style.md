When it comes to home decor, sometimes less is more, but many homeowners unknowingly commit design blunders that make their spaces feel cheap and uninviting. Fortunately, you don’t need to splurge on expensive pieces to create an elevated look. By avoiding these three common mistakes identified by interior designers, you can stylishly upgrade your home without blowing your budget.

## 1. Don’t Settle for Small Rugs

One of the most common pitfalls in home styling is opting for rugs that are too small for the space. A small rug can make a room feel disjointed and cheapen its overall look. Instead, aim for a larger area rug that allows all the furniture to sit comfortably on it. The right-sized rug should ideally anchor the furniture and create a cohesive look. 

For example, if you’re furnishing your living room, choose a rug that extends beyond the front legs of all seating arrangements. This creates a unified space that feels intentional and luxurious. 

### Quick Tip:
When shopping for rugs, consider layering them if you have a smaller budget. A large neutral rug underneath can serve as a base, while a colorful or textured smaller rug on top can add interest.

## 2. Don’t Deprioritize Practical Elements

Elevating your decor doesn’t stop at aesthetics; practical elements also play a crucial role in how your room feels. Items like proper lighting, adequate storage, and well-placed furniture can enhance both the usability and visual appeal of the space. 

Skimping on these essentials can make a room feel chaotic or unfinished. For instance, ensure you have enough light sources to create a warm ambiance. Use a mix of ambient, task, and accent lighting to add depth and warmth. 

### Quick Tip:
Conceal unsightly cords and wires with decorative cord covers or by strategically placing furniture in a way that minimizes their visibility. This not only tidies up your space but also avoids the cluttered look that cheapens a room.  

## 3. Don’t Go for Grommet-Top Curtains

Curtains can either enhance or detract from your home’s style, depending on their design. Grommet-top curtains have become a trendy choice, but they often lack the elegance and custom look that more traditional styles offer. Instead of grommets, consider opting for tailored drapes with rod pockets or pleats that add sophistication and visual height to your windows. 

### Quick Tip:
Choose curtains that extend all the way to the floor for a high-end look. This trick creates an illusion of taller ceilings, making the space feel more open and inviting.

## Conclusion

By steering clear of these three decor mistakes—using oversized rugs, prioritizing practical elements, and choosing elegant curtain styles—you can elevate your home’s appearance without the need for high-cost upgrades. Focus on intention and thoughtful styling and watch your spaces transform without breaking the bank. Create a home that not only feels sophisticated but also reflects your personal style and comfort.
