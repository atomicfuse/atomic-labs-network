TL;DR: As we move towards 2026, old money colors like teal and chocolate are set to take over from classic coastal shades. Here’s how to embrace these rich tones in your home while keeping it elegant and cozy.

## The Shift in Interior Design Trends

If you’ve been feeling a bit uninspired by the endless palette of coastal navy and beige in home decor, you’re not alone! In fact, the 2026 interior design trends are taking a delightful turn towards richer, more inviting colors. Inspired by the striking hues used in Kathy Bates’ Los Angeles living room, this new direction highlights the cozy, luxurious feel of "old money" tones like teal and chocolate brown.

Gone are the days of minimalism that left many homes feeling stark and cold. Instead, we’re leaning into emotionally secure spaces that make us feel wrapped in warmth and comfort. Ready to embrace the new trend? Let’s dive into how you can redefine your space with these elegant, timeless hues.

## The Allure of Old Money Colors

### Timeless and Characterful

So what exactly is the appeal of these richer colors? Think about the warmth of deep browns paired with icy teal—the combinations feel both timeless and characterful, offering a striking contrast that can truly elevate your space. This palette isn’t just about making a statement; it’s about creating an atmosphere where you can truly feel at home.

### Why Teal and Chocolate?

Teal and chocolate brown create a harmonious balance that feels both inviting and sophisticated. These colors can ground a space while still allowing for creativity in styling. Whether it's a grand living room or a cozy study nook, these shades can enhance the vibe while making the area feel luxurious rather than heavy.

## How to Incorporate Old Money Hues in Your Home

### Start Small: Accent Pieces

You don’t have to completely overhaul your decor to embrace this trend. Start by introducing accent pieces in teal and chocolate. Think about:
- **Throw pillows** in rich, textured fabrics.
- **Art pieces** that showcase these colors.
- **An area rug** that pulls everything together.

These small additions can make a big impact without needing a full decor revamp.

### Upgrade Your Furniture

If you're ready to invest in new furniture, consider pieces in chocolate upholstery or rich teal accents. A chocolate brown sofa can serve as a beautiful focal point, especially when paired with lighter accents for contrast. Don’t shy away from bold styling—layering textures like leather, velvet, and natural fibers will create depth and interest.

### Wall Colors That Speak Volumes

If you feel adventurous, why not paint an accent wall in a deep teal? This daring change can instantly transform your space, making it feel sophisticated and cozy. If painting feels like too much, opt for removable wallpaper that features these colors for a temporary yet impactful change.

## Curating the Right Accessories

### Decorate with Intention

Accessories can truly make or break a space. To complement your new color palette:
- Use **artwork** that reflects the warm tones of chocolate and teal, perhaps with nature scenes that speak to these rich hues.
- Choose **metallic accents** in gold or brass that pair beautifully with these colors, adding a touch of elegance.
- Incorporate **natural materials** like wood and stone for a grounded feel that harmonizes with the old money aesthetic.

### Rethink Lighting

Lighting plays a crucial role in how colors are perceived. Soft, warm light can elevate the richness of chocolate and turquoise tones. Consider vintage-style lampshades or warm LED bulbs to enhance the cozy atmosphere of your space.

## Balancing Rich Colors for Elegance

### Avoiding Overwhelm

When embracing deeper hues, it’s essential to strike a balance to avoid a heavy or dark feel in your room. Here are some tips:
- Pair dark colors with lighter elements like white or beige to keep the area feeling bright and inviting.
- Keep the distribution of rich colors varied throughout the room to create visual interest without overwhelming any single area.
- Opt for large windows or mirrors to reflect light and enhance the openness of your space.

## Conclusion

So there you have it! Embracing rich old money colors like teal and chocolate can elevate your home decor, making it not only stylish but also warm and inviting. As we move into 2026, these colors are set to provide a cozy alternative to the classic coastal themes we’ve become accustomed to. Remember, it’s all about creating a space where you feel comforted and at ease, and with these tips, you’ll be well on your way to achieving that perfect balance of elegance and coziness. Happy decorating!
