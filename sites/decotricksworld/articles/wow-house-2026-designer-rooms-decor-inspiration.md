## Prestigious Design Showcase Returns for Fifth Year

WOW!house is back for its fifth year at Design Centre Chelsea Harbour, according to reports, bringing together 22 rooms and outdoor spaces that showcase the latest in interior design innovation. The prestigious showcase features work from both established and emerging designers from around the globe, offering homeowners a wealth of inspiration for their own decorating projects.

## Diverse Design Inspirations Under One Roof

This year's exhibition spans an impressive range of design styles and spaces. According to reports, visitors can explore everything from Georgian drawing rooms and Mediterranean courtyards to sculptural libraries and luxury bars. The showcase demonstrates how different architectural influences and design philosophies can be adapted for contemporary living.

The featured spaces include a Garden Folly Facade by Darren Price of Adam Architecture, alongside a complementary Garden Folly Room created by Studio Enass. The Gardenists have contributed an Artorius Faber Entrance Garden, while another facade design by Price showcases the Size Group's aesthetic approach.

## Notable Designer Contributions

Several standout spaces highlight the diversity of design talent featured in this year's showcase. Francis Sultana has designed the Entrance Hall, setting the tone for visitors as they explore the exhibition. The Turnell & Gigon Group Drawing Room, created by Albion Nord, offers insights into contemporary approaches to classic living spaces.

Roisin Lafferty has contributed the Shepel' Library, which according to reports takes on a sculpture-like quality that reimagines how we think about home library spaces. Meanwhile, Elicyon has designed the Lalique Home Bar, described as "London's coolest speakeasy," demonstrating how luxury entertaining spaces can be incorporated into residential design.
