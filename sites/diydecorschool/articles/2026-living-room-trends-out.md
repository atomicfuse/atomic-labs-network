TL;DR: Designers are moving away from lifeless living room trends like all-beige palettes and hidden TVs. Instead, prioritize warmth, personality, and functionality in your space.

## Trends Designers Are Saying Goodbye To
In the ever-changing world of interior design, trends come and go like the seasons. As we look ahead to 2026, industry experts are already waving goodbye to certain living room features that they feel are becoming outdated. Let’s dive into those trends and explore what you can do instead to create a vibrant, inviting space.

### 1. The Neutral RH Aesthetic
Gone are the days of cold, neutral tones dominating our living spaces. The all-beige interiors are feeling particularly tired and sterile. Instead of following the route of a monotonous color palette, consider integrating shades that reflect warmth. **Warm earth tones**, **soft pastels**, or even a splash of bold color can create an atmosphere that invites comfort.

### 2. Hidden TVs
While the trend of hiding the television to achieve a clean look may have gained popularity, it is now seen as restricting the functionality of your space. TVs are part of modern living. **Opt for stylish media consoles** or wall-mounted options that blend with your decor. Consider artistic shelving around the TV to display your favorite items and make your entertainment center a focal point rather than a hidden distraction.

### 3. An Industrial Influence
The industrial vibe that once brought a chic touch to many homes is increasingly feeling out of place. Instead of stark, cold materials, it's time to foster a softer interior. Introduce **textured fabrics**, **natural materials**, and **vintage elements** that provide a sense of character and warmth. Combine local craftsmanship with a splash of your personal style to give your space a unique narrative.

### 4. Rounded Bouclé Chairs and Sofas
While bouclé fabric was once the darling of furniture design, many attribute its impracticality to its current decline. If you want to keep your space stylish yet functional, consider **layering fabrics** with more durability while also playing with different textures. A mix of materials like leather and cotton not only adds character but also increases the practicality of your furniture pieces.

### 5. Gallery Wall Grids
Yes, gallery walls were the ultimate statement of artistic expression, but surging trends are dragging them down to cliché. The solution? Rethink how you display art. Approach the gallery wall with **mixed frames, diverse art styles, and dimensional pieces**. Even board games or wall sculptures can bring in personality, breaking the monotony of grid organization and inviting a more collected feel.

### 6. Matching Wood Tones
Finally, saying goodbye to matching wood tones is about embracing individuality over uniformity. Many designers are pushing for varied wood textures and shades, which showcases the uniqueness of each piece. Mix and match different woods—like pairing dark walnut with light oak—to create stunning contrasts and rich depth in your living area.

## Embrace Change for a More Personal Space
Home design trends may be fleeting, but your home should stand as a reflection of who you are. As we anticipate the style of 2026, think beyond the seasonality of trends. Instead, aim for vibrant, personal designs that feel comfortable and inviting. 

Instead of following the herd, allow your creativity to express itself in every detail of your living room. By embracing color, mixing and matching wood tones, and featuring pieces that tell a story, your space will not only feel more alive, it will resonate with your personality. Keep these trends in mind as you breathe new life into your home, ensuring it remains warm, welcoming, and truly yours.
