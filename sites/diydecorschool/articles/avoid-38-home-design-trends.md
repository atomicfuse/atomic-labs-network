TL;DR: Many new home design trends prioritize aesthetics over functionality. Here are 38 trends homeowners are increasingly pushing back against for a more practical living space.

## Introduction
In today’s Instagram-obsessed world, it’s easy to get swept up in the latest home design trends. However, what looks stunning in glossy magazine photos may not make for a comfortable, functional home. From impractical layouts to unsafe choices, it turns out that many modern design trends aren't all they’re cracked up to be. Let's explore 38 of the most controversial trends currently causing a stir in home design circles.

## 1. Removing Stair Banisters
Minimalism has its advantages, but removing stair banisters is a step too far for safety's sake. Not only does it open up the space visually, but it also increases the risk of falls, especially for kids and older adults. Let's prioritize safety in our homes!

## 2. Open Concept Bathrooms
While open concept living can make a space feel larger, open bathrooms? Not so much. Privacy takes a hit, and let’s not even talk about the awkwardness of sharing a bathroom space with guests.

## 3. Too Many Pot Lights
Pot lights can create a wash of light, but too many can feel harsh and sterile. Finding that perfect balance is key, and relying solely on these fixtures can lead to an uninspired ambiance.

## 4. Too Many Bathrooms
We love convenience as much as anyone, but are six bathrooms really necessary? Overloading on bathrooms can lead to absurd layouts that waste valuable space.

## 5. Agreeable Gray Everywhere
The charming neutral palette of Agreeable Gray has taken the world by storm, but its overuse can feel bland. Consider mixing in some unique colors to keep your home vibrant and full of character.

## 6. Lack of Storage Space
Trendy open layouts often come at the cost of storage. With nowhere to stash your belongings, your home can quickly turn into a cluttered mess. Let’s prioritize smart storage solutions instead!

## 7. No Door Between Primary Bedroom and Bathroom
Say goodbye to privacy and hello to awkward moments! A door separating the bathroom from the bedroom isn’t just functional; it's necessary for personal comfort.

## 8. Glass Railings on Stairs or Decks
While glass railings might seem sleek and modern, they are often a cleaning nightmare. Smudges and fingerprints can ruin the aesthetic, making it a less-than-ideal choice for those who want low-maintenance features.

## 9. Built-In Appliances Integrated Into Walls
These can be a striking design choice, but they also come with high costs if repairs are needed. Often, this trend prioritizes style over function, not to mention the potential headaches during renovations.

## 10. Beautiful Front Exteriors with Cheap Vinyl Backs
Nothing screams ‘poor-quality build’ more than a stunning façade hiding flimsy construction. Maintaining a consistent level of quality throughout the home is essential for lasting value.

## 11. Modern Farmhouse Style
The modern farmhouse trend captured the hearts of many, but it also invites clichés and overuse. Consider alternative styles that reflect your personal vibe without the risk of blending into the crowd.

## 12. Floor-to-Ceiling Clear Glass Showers
These can be striking but make cleaning a bear. Not to mention, privacy can be a major issue for households with multiple people. Sometimes a well-placed curtain does the job better!

## 13. Microwave 'Vent Hoods'
Not only do they look less appealing, but vent hoods that double as microwaves often lack efficiency. It’s worth investing in well-designed appliances that perform their functions well.

## 14. Narrow Rectangle Kitchens
While some may think efficient, a narrow kitchen can feel cramped. Keep your workflow in mind and design a space that allows for comfortable cooking and movement.

## 15. Humongous Kitchen Islands
While an expansive kitchen island can be a gathering point, oversized ones can dominate the room and make the kitchen feel unbalanced. Opt for proportionate designs that still allow for functionality.

## 16. Huge Front-Facing Windows
These may look stunning, but privacy and temperature control might suffer. Think about the practicality of large windows before committing to this design trend.

## 17. Counter-Depth Fridges
While visually appealing, they tend to come at a premium price and might sacrifice storage space. Consider your culinary needs before going for this investment.

## 18. Badly Designed Sinks
Let’s get real: a beautiful sink isn’t worth it if it's impractical. Opt for designs that cater to daily use rather than just aesthetics.

## 19. Overdone Gray-and-White Minimalism
While this color scheme is trendy, it can feel cold and lifeless if overused. Inject some warmth and color into your life with bolder choices!

## 20. Open Shelving Instead of Cabinetry
While it looks cute on Instagram, open shelving can quickly become cluttered and disorganized. If your kitchen can’t stay clean, consider good ol’ cabinetry instead.

## 21. No Broom Closets
A broom closet may seem trivial, but lacking storage for cleaning supplies is more inconvenient than you may think. Everyone needs a dedicated space for these essentials!

## 22. TVs Mounted Over Fireplaces
While visually appealing in theory, this setup can make viewing awkward and uncomfortable. Consider your furniture layout and comfort when mounting a TV.

## 23. Too-Small Two-Car Garages
If you plan to park two vehicles, ensure your garage offers enough space. Fitting more than one vehicle in cramped quarters can turn your dream home into a nightly challenge.

## 24. Painting Over Brick
Sure, it can freshen things up, but painted brick can be a nightmare to repaint later. Embrace the beauty and texture of natural brick instead.

## 25. Tiny Laundry Rooms, Pantries, and Closets
Small spaces can work, but only if they are well designed. Squishing everything into tiny areas can be a huge inconvenience. Prioritize space planning!

## Conclusion
As you plan your next renovation or home purchase, keep in mind that trendy features may not translate into a practical and enjoyable living experience. Aim for comfort, practicality, and a design that fits your lifestyle instead of just following the latest aesthetic. After all, your home should be a sanctuary, not just a magazine spread.
