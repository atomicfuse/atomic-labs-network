## Discovering the Latest Trends at WOW!house
London’s WOW!house isn’t just a beautiful display of homes; it’s a treasure trove of the latest interior design trends that elevate everyday living. From the verdigris effect to the return of damask, let’s explore how these trends can transform your home while keeping it personal and modern.

## 1. The Verdigris Effect
The verdigris effect, inspired by the natural patina of copper and bronze, adds an elegant touch to any interior. This finish brings depth and warmth, allowing creative touches without overwhelming your existing decor. You might wonder how to integrate this stunning trend without creating an overly formal space. 

### How to Use Verdigris Finishes in Your Home
- **Accent Pieces:** Consider incorporating verdigris through smaller items such as candle holders or decorative bowls. These pieces can add a hint of sophistication without going overboard.
- **Wall Art:** Art featuring verdigris hues can serve as a beautiful focal point in living rooms or dining areas.
- **Furniture:** If you're feeling adventurous, look for furniture pieces with a verdigris finish. Items like side tables can ease you into the trend, blending the old with the contemporary.

## 2. Damask Is Back (Yes, Really!)
If you thought damask was a thing of the past, think again! This classic textile is making a grand comeback, weaving its intricate patterns into modern designs. Damask brings an air of opulence but can be easily adapted for everyday use.

### How to Style Damask in Your Decor
- **Soft Furnishings:** Incorporate damask through cushions, curtains, or throws. Opt for subtle colors if you want a more contemporary look.
- **Feature Walls:** Create a show-stopping feature wall with damask wallpaper. This will enhance visual interest without overwhelming the space.
- **Layering Textures:** Pair damask with simpler fabrics like linen or cotton to keep your space feeling fresh and not overly ornate.

## 3. Architectural Tailoring
Architectural tailoring is about transforming the way we view spaces. This trend focuses on integrating fabric-wrapped details, which adds a softer touch to hard architectural features. It’s a practical way to create warmth and comfort in sleek environments.

### Making Architectural Tailoring Work for You
- **Fabric-Wrapped Panels:** Try wrapping panels in a luxurious fabric to soften walls or feature architectural elements. This can be a fantastic DIY project for those willing to get crafty!
- **Softening Corners:** Consider fabric details in unexpected places, like cornices or columns, to introduce visual softness.
- **Mix Styles:** Balance modern lines with traditional wraps to create a layered look that feels inviting and chic.
