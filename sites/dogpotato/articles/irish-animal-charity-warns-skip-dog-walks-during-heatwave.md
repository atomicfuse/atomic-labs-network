As Ireland braces for potentially record-breaking May temperatures, the Irish Blue Cross has issued urgent guidance warning pet owners against exercising their dogs during hot weather. With temperatures expected to exceed 20°C, animal welfare experts are emphasizing that many pet owners continue to underestimate the serious dangers that heatwaves pose to their beloved companions.

According to reports, the warning comes at a critical time as Ireland may hit record May temperatures, making pet heat safety an urgent seasonal concern that requires immediate attention from all dog owners.

## The 20°C Risk Rule: When Walking Becomes Dangerous

The Irish Blue Cross guidance highlights a crucial threshold that many pet owners overlook: temperatures above 20°C can pose significant risks to dogs during exercise. This temperature marker serves as an important guideline for when normal walking routines should be suspended in favor of keeping pets safe and cool.

Animal welfare experts stress that walks, hot pavements, and parked cars become particularly dangerous once this temperature threshold is passed, creating conditions that can quickly lead to serious health complications for dogs of all sizes and breeds.
