## Young Lab Takes on Life-Saving Mission

Mando may look like any other energetic young Labrador retriever, but this 18-month-old canine has taken on one of the most important jobs in emergency services. The Springfield Township Fire Department has certified Mando as a human remains detection dog, also known as a cadaver dog, to help solve missing persons cases and provide closure to grieving families.

## Filling Critical Resource Gap

According to reports, Mando's certification addresses a significant need in northwest Ohio, where specialized detection dogs were previously unavailable locally. Before Mando joined the team, emergency services had to call in cadaver dogs from locations 2-3 hours away, creating delays that could prove crucial in time-sensitive investigations.

The young Lab's handler, Shelbie Flegal, brings multiple qualifications to this specialized role. Flegal serves as a firefighter and paramedic while also operating as a small business owner who trains cadaver dogs. This combination of emergency services experience and specialized animal training expertise makes her uniquely qualified to work with detection dogs.

## Rigorous Training from Puppyhood

Mando's preparation for this critical work began early in his life. According to reports, cadaver dog training requires intensive commitment, with handlers dedicating 5-10+ hours weekly to training sessions that start when puppies are just 8 weeks old. This extensive preparation ensures the dogs develop the precision and reliability needed for their important work.

The training process transforms what appears to be a typical family pet into a highly specialized working animal capable of detecting human remains that might otherwise go undiscovered. While Mando exhibits the playful energy typical of young Labs, his specialized training enables him to perform searches that can make the difference between unresolved cases and bringing answers to families.

## Emotional Impact on Communities

Missing persons cases and unresolved deaths create lasting emotional devastation for families and communities. The presence of a local cadaver dog team means faster response times and more immediate support for families facing their worst fears. Rather than waiting hours for specialized resources to arrive from distant locations, local emergency services can now deploy Mando and his handler quickly when time may be of the essence.
