## The Quest for Perfect Pakode

The beloved combination of crispy pakoda and chai remains unbeatable, especially during rainy weather and snack cravings. However, many home cooks struggle with a common problem: their pakodas lose texture and never achieve the crispy, crunchy consistency they're after. According to reports, people often complain that their homemade pakodas turn out soggy and overly oily.

MasterChef Pankaj Bhadouria recently addressed this widespread kitchen challenge by sharing five smart techniques that can help achieve street-style pakode texture at home while significantly reducing oil absorption.

## Five Essential Techniques for Better Pakode

### Avoid Water and Perfect Your Whisking

The foundation of crispy pakode starts with proper batter preparation. Chef Bhadouria emphasizes avoiding water in the batter mixture and focusing on thorough whisking technique. This fundamental step helps create the right consistency for achieving that coveted crunch.

### Incorporate Rice Flour for Extra Crispiness

One of the key secrets to restaurant-quality pakode lies in adding rice flour to your batter. This ingredient acts as a natural crisping agent, helping create that satisfying textural contrast between the crispy exterior and fluffy interior that makes pakode so appealing.

### Add Baking Soda for Fluffiness

To achieve the light, fluffy texture that characterizes perfect pakode, Chef Bhadouria recommends incorporating baking soda into the batter. This leavening agent helps create air pockets during frying, resulting in pakode that are less dense and more enjoyable to eat.

### The Hot Oil Addition Trick

A professional technique that many home cooks overlook involves adding hot oil directly to the batter. This method helps pre-cook parts of the mixture and contributes to achieving better texture and reduced oil absorption during the actual frying process.

### Master the Oil Temperature Test

Perhaps the most crucial factor in creating less oily pakode is maintaining proper frying temperature. Chef Bhadouria emphasizes the importance of checking and controlling oil temperature throughout the cooking process. The right temperature ensures that pakode cook quickly on the outside, forming a protective barrier that prevents excessive oil absorption while allowing the interior to cook properly.
