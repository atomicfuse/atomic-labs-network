## Industry Unity Crumbles Over Prediction Markets Dispute

The American Gaming Association (AGA) has experienced a dramatic reshuffling of its membership over the past six months, with four major operators—DraftKings, FanDuel, Fanatics, and bet365—departing the organization due to disagreements over prediction markets. This exodus has fractured what was once a unified lobbying voice for the gambling industry, according to reports.

The timing of these departures is particularly significant as Congress prepares for its first major hearing on prediction markets, scheduled for May 20 in the Senate. This hearing will serve as the initial test of how the newly realigned lobbying landscape will function in Washington.

## Competing Visions for Market Regulation

At the heart of the dispute lies a fundamental disagreement about the role of prediction markets in the broader gambling ecosystem. AGA president and CEO Bill Miller has framed prediction markets as encroachment on legal, state-regulated and tribal-regulated operators, highlighting the tension between traditional gaming interests and emerging market platforms.

While the AGA maintains its focus on protecting established gambling operations, prediction-market platforms like Kalshi and Polymarket are building their own coalition. According to reports, these platforms are partnering with crypto heavyweights and investing millions of dollars to defend their market position.

## Legislative Battle Lines Form

The stakes extend beyond industry politics, as multiple bills are already circulating in Congress that could impose restrictions on sports prediction contracts. The outcome of the May 20 Senate hearing and subsequent legislative actions could determine whether prediction markets establish themselves as a legitimate gaming vertical or face regulatory constraints that could severely limit their operations.

This legislative uncertainty creates a complex environment for industry participants, with some operators finding themselves caught in the middle of competing interests. Companies like BetMGM must navigate between traditional gambling regulation and the evolving prediction markets landscape.
