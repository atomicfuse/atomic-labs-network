## Classified Intel Meets Crypto Gambling

A US Army special forces soldier now faces federal charges after allegedly exploiting classified military information to win more than $400,000 on cryptocurrency prediction markets. According to the Justice Department, Gannon Ken Van Dyke used inside knowledge of Operation Absolute Resolve—the classified mission to capture Venezuelan leader Nicolas Maduro—to place strategic bets before the operation became public knowledge.

The case represents a troubling intersection of national security vulnerabilities and the rapidly expanding world of decentralized finance, raising urgent questions about how military secrets can be weaponized for personal profit in crypto markets.

## How the Scheme Unfolded

According to reports, Van Dyke allegedly leveraged his access to classified information about the Venezuelan operation to make calculated trades on Polymarket, a popular crypto-powered prediction platform. The soldier's betting strategy reportedly focused on outcomes related to Nicolas Maduro's removal from power, positioning him to profit substantially when the classified operation eventually became public.

The Justice Department's investigation reveals how Van Dyke accumulated winnings exceeding $400,000 through these trades, demonstrating the significant financial incentives that could motivate military personnel to compromise national security for personal gain.
