TL;DR: Want perfectly crispy moong dal pakode? Follow these five easy tips from Chef Pankaj Bhadouria to elevate your snack game!

### The Delight of Moong Dal Pakode
Whether it’s a rainy day or just an afternoon snack craving, nothing beats the comfort of crispy moong dal pakode paired with chai. However, many home cooks struggle with achieving the perfect crunch without excess oil. Fear not! Chef Pankaj Bhadouria has shared five invaluable tips to help you master this beloved Indian snack.

### 1. Avoid Water and Whisk Well
One of the common mistakes when preparing pakode is adding too much water to the batter. Instead, strive for a thick consistency! This allows the batter to stick to the dal, ensuring wonderful, fluffy pakode. 

**Pro Tip:** Whisk your batter thoroughly to incorporate air, lending a light and fluffy texture to your pakode. The more air you can whip into your batter, the better the final texture! 

### 2. Use Rice Flour for Extra Crunch
For a truly crispy bite, consider adding rice flour to your moong dal batter. Rice flour not only enhances the crunchiness but also helps in reducing oil absorption during frying.

**Mix It Up:** You can use a ratio of 2:1 or 3:1 for moong dal to rice flour to boost that crisp factor while still keeping the delectable taste of lentils intact.

### 3. Add Baking Soda
Another trick to achieving the ultimate texture is to sprinkle in a small amount of baking soda to your batter. This will help aerate the batter, making your pakode light and fluffy.

**How Much?** Just a pinch is sufficient! Remember, too much baking soda can affect the taste, so moderation is key.

### 4. Incorporate Hot Oil Into the Batter
A game-changing tip from Chef Pankaj is to add hot oil directly into your batter before frying. This not only helps in crispiness but also in making the pakode less oily!

**Why It Works:** The hot oil heats the batter instantly, forming a seal that keeps moisture inside and creates a crispy outer layer. 

### 5. Check the Oil Temperature
Maintaining the right oil temperature is critical for frying crispy pakode. Begin with oil heated to around 350°F (180°C). If the oil isn’t hot enough, the pakode will absorb oil, becoming soggy instead of crispy.

**Tip for Testing Oil:** Drop a small amount of batter into the oil; it should sizzle and rise to the top immediately. If it sinks, the oil is too cold.

### Conclusion: Snack Away with Confidence!
With these five expert tips from Chef Pankaj Bhadouria, you’re now equipped to create crispy moong dal pakode that rivals your favorite street vendor's. Perfectly paired with chai, these pakode will be a hit during snack time, any day of the week.

So, gather your ingredients and give it a go! Your taste buds will thank you!
