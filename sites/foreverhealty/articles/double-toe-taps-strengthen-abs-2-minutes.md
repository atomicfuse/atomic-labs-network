## What Are Double Toe Taps?
Double toe taps are an advanced Pilates exercise that specifically targets your lower abdominals and deep stabilizing muscles. When you perform this exercise correctly, you can expect to improve your core strength, enhance posture, and even alleviate lower back pain over time. Plus, it’s a low-impact move suitable for all fitness levels!

## How to Perform Double Toe Taps
To maximize the benefits, it’s essential to focus on proper form when performing double toe taps. Here’s a step-by-step guide to doing them correctly:

### Step 1: Start Position
Lie on your back with your legs in a tabletop position (knees bent at 90 degrees). Your feet should be hip-width apart, and your arms should be resting at your sides.

### Step 2: Engage Your Core
Pull your belly button towards your spine—a crucial step for activating your core muscles.

### Step 3: Lower Your Legs
Slowly lower both legs towards the floor, keeping your back pressed against the mat. Aim to lower them as far as you can without arching your lower back. 

### Step 4: Return to Start
Bring your legs back to the tabletop position while engaging your core throughout the movement.

### Step 5: Repeat
Aim for 10-15 repetitions, remembering to keep your movements controlled and focused. If double toe taps feel too challenging at first, try starting with single toe taps until you build strength.
