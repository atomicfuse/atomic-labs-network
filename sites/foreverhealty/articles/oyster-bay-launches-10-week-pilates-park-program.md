## Free Outdoor Pilates Classes Coming to Theodore Roosevelt Park

Oyster Bay residents looking to combine fitness with fresh air this summer have a new opportunity to look forward to. Town Supervisor Joseph Saladino recently announced that registration will soon open for the Town's Pilates in the Park program, offering community members a chance to participate in outdoor wellness activities.

The 10-week Saturday fitness series is scheduled to begin July 11 and will run from 10:00 to 11:00 a.m. each week at Theodore Roosevelt Park. This community-based program represents part of the town's summer programming efforts to provide accessible wellness activities for residents.

## What Makes This Program Special

According to reports, Supervisor Saladino emphasized that the program balances "wellness and athleticism," highlighting Pilates as an ideal exercise format for outdoor community fitness. The choice of Theodore Roosevelt Park as the venue provides participants with a scenic natural setting for their weekly wellness routine.

The Saturday morning time slot is strategically chosen to accommodate various schedules while taking advantage of cooler morning temperatures during the summer months. The consistent weekly format allows participants to build a sustainable fitness habit over the 10-week period.

## Benefits of Outdoor Pilates Practice

Pilates is recognized as a low-impact exercise method that can benefit participants of various fitness levels and ages. The practice focuses on core strength, flexibility, and body awareness, making it an accessible form of exercise for community members who may be new to fitness or returning after a break.

Combining Pilates with an outdoor park setting offers additional wellness benefits. Fresh air and natural surroundings can enhance the mind-body connection that Pilates emphasizes, while the community aspect of group classes provides social interaction and motivation.
