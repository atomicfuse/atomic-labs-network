The primary mirror showcases extreme precision engineering that pushes the boundaries of current technology. This level of precision engineering represents decades of development behind this single component, highlighting the complex challenges involved in creating instruments capable of operating in the harsh environment of space while maintaining the accuracy needed for groundbreaking scientific observations.

The Roman Space Telescope's infrared capabilities are designed to differ from and complement the James Webb Space Telescope, according to reports.
