## Peloton Expands Into Premium Pilates Market

Peloton has acquired Skōp, the maker of high-end connected Pilates reformers priced at nearly $8,000, according to reports. This strategic acquisition signals the fitness company's push into the Pilates equipment market and represents a significant expansion beyond its core cycling business into broader connected home fitness categories.

The move comes as Peloton faces ongoing challenges with subscriber saturation and increased competition in the connected fitness space, prompting the company to diversify its product portfolio beyond its signature bikes and treadmills.

## The Premium Pilates Equipment Boom

The acquisition taps into what reports describe as an exploding Pilates reformer market, particularly among affluent fitness enthusiasts seeking premium, tech-enabled workout equipment. Skōp's nearly $8,000 price point positions it squarely in the luxury segment of home fitness equipment, targeting consumers willing to invest significantly in connected workout technology.

According to industry observations, the Pilates reformer market has experienced remarkable growth, with high-end equipment becoming increasingly mainstream among consumers seeking sophisticated home fitness solutions.

## Strategic Pivot Beyond Cardio

This acquisition represents Peloton's broader strategy to expand connected fitness beyond traditional cardio equipment into strength and flexibility training categories. The move demonstrates how connected fitness companies are evolving to offer more comprehensive workout ecosystems rather than focusing solely on single equipment categories.

The Pilates market presents unique challenges for Peloton, as it has traditionally been dominated by boutique brands like Reformer Pilates and Lagree. Reports suggest this acquisition will test whether a mega-brand like Peloton can successfully compete in what has historically been a niche category controlled by specialized manufacturers.

## Technology and Expertise Focus

According to reports, the acquisition is specifically targeted at Skōp's technology and expertise rather than just its product line. This suggests Peloton is interested in the underlying connected fitness technology that powers Skōp's reformers, potentially indicating plans to integrate these capabilities across its broader equipment ecosystem.

For existing Peloton subscribers, this acquisition could signal new class offerings and ecosystem expansion beyond the company's current cycling and running content.
