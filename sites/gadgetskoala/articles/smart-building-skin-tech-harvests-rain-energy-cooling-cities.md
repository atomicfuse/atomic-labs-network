## Revolutionary Building Technology Tackles Urban Energy Challenges

City University of Hong Kong has unveiled two groundbreaking sustainable technologies that could transform how cities approach energy efficiency and clean fuel production. The innovations come at a critical time as urban centers worldwide struggle to meet carbon neutrality goals while managing rising energy costs.

The university's JC STEM Lab of Circular Bio-economy has developed the BRIDGE skin, a paintable building coating that simultaneously cools structures and harvests energy from rainfall. This bio-inspired solution draws from nature's own engineering, specifically mimicking Tillandsia air plants to solve complex trade-offs in building coating design.

## Nature-Inspired Engineering Breakthrough

According to reports, the BRIDGE skin technology represents a significant advancement in bio-inspired design, addressing real-world climate problems through solutions found in nature. The coating's ability to provide dual functionality—temperature regulation and energy generation—makes it particularly valuable for retrofitting existing urban infrastructure.

Unlike rigid solar panels, the paintable nature of BRIDGE skin offers crucial advantages for urban adoption. The technology can be applied to existing buildings without major structural modifications, making it accessible for widespread deployment across dense urban environments like Hong Kong and the Greater Bay Area.

## Copper Breakthrough Democratizes Hydrogen Production

Alongside the building skin innovation, the research team has developed a copper-ion-based hydrogen production system that could revolutionize clean fuel accessibility. According to reports, this system generates hydrogen continuously and affordably without relying on expensive platinum catalysts.

The copper-ion breakthrough represents a significant shift in hydrogen production economics. By replacing platinum with abundant copper materials, the technology could democratize hydrogen production and make clean fuel generation more accessible globally.

## Round-the-Clock Clean Energy Solution

The hydrogen production system addresses one of renewable energy's biggest challenges by operating continuously, both in darkness and daylight. According to reports, this round-the-clock capability ensures consistent clean energy production regardless of weather conditions or time of day.

This continuous operation model aligns with circular economy principles, creating closed-loop systems that reduce waste while maintaining self-sustaining energy production. The technology's ability to function independently of solar conditions makes it particularly valuable for regions experiencing energy market volatility.

## Urban Application and Scalability

Both technologies target urgent urban sustainability challenges, particularly relevant for high-density regions facing climate pressures and energy market instability. The paintable coating's retrofitting capabilities mean existing building stock can be upgraded without extensive renovation projects.

According to reports, these innovations address practical implications of making green energy accessible and scalable through cost-effective materials. The technologies represent how circular bio-economy principles can be applied to create real-world solutions for urban environmental challenges.

## Impact on Carbon Neutrality Goals

As cities worldwide race to meet ambitious carbon reduction targets, these technologies offer practical pathways for immediate implementation. The BRIDGE skin coating provides buildings with passive cooling capabilities while generating renewable energy, potentially reducing both energy consumption and carbon emissions simultaneously.

The affordable hydrogen production system complements this approach by providing clean fuel alternatives that don't require expensive infrastructure investments.
