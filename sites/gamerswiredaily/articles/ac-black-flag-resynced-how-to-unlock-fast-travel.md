If you're diving back into *Assassin's Creed IV: Black Flag* through the *Resynced* version and feeling frustrated by the sheer size of the Caribbean map, you're not alone. Fast travel is one of the biggest quality-of-life features in the game — but it isn't available from the start. Here's exactly when you unlock it, how each method works, and how to use it wisely.

## When Does Fast Travel Unlock?

Fast travel in *AC Black Flag Resynced* becomes available after completing **"This Tyro Captain"** in **Sequence 3**. Until you hit that story milestone, you're locked into sailing, swimming, or running everywhere on foot — which can eat up serious time early in the game.

This is one of the most common beginner mistakes players make: spending time trying to explore and collect items across the map before fast travel is even an option. If you're just getting started, prioritize pushing through the story to Sequence 3 to unlock this feature as quickly as possible. It will make every subsequent session — whether you're hunting collectibles, clearing side missions, or mopping up the map — significantly faster.

## The Three Fast Travel Methods

Once you've completed "This Tyro Captain," three distinct methods of fast travel open up. Each one serves a slightly different purpose depending on where you are and where you need to go.

### 1. Viewpoints

Synchronizing with viewpoints has always been a staple of the *Assassin's Creed* series, and *Black Flag Resynced* is no different. After syncing a viewpoint, you can use it as a fast travel destination. This is especially useful for jumping between major islands and locations you've already explored. The more viewpoints you sync early on, the more flexibility you'll have when navigating the open world later.

### 2. The World Map

The in-game world map also functions as a fast travel hub. Once locations are unlocked, you can travel directly to them from the map screen. This is arguably the most convenient method for long-distance navigation, particularly when you need to get from one side of the Caribbean to the other without spending several minutes at the helm of the *Jackdaw*.

### 3. Taverns

Taverns scattered across the game world offer a third fast travel option. These locations serve as quick hops between nearby areas and can be particularly handy when you're deep into a region and need to reposition quickly without pulling up the full world map.

## Why Fast Travel Matters in Resynced

With players returning to *Black Flag* through the *Resynced* release, fast travel is more relevant than ever. The Caribbean is a massive open world filled with side content, collectibles, and optional missions — all of which are far more approachable once you're not spending half your session just getting from point A to point B.

For players focused on exploration and 100% completion, fast travel is essentially a requirement. Hunting down shanties, Animus fragments, and naval contracts across dozens of islands becomes a much more manageable task when you can jump between discovered locations instantly.

## Tips for Smarter Navigation

Here are a few practical pointers to get the most out of fast travel once it unlocks:

- **Sync viewpoints early and often.** The more locations you unlock, the more fast travel destinations you'll have available. Make syncing a priority whenever you arrive somewhere new.
- **Don't over-explore before Sequence 3.** Sailing to remote corners of the map before fast travel is unlocked means you'll likely have to make that same trip again later. Save heavy exploration for after the feature is available.
- **Use the world map for long distances.** When you need to cover a lot of ground quickly, the world map is your fastest option. Taverns are better suited for shorter hops within a region.
- **Plan collection runs efficiently.** Once fast travel is active, you can plan out collectible hunts by region rather than wandering aimlessly. Focus on one area, clear it out, then fast travel to the next.

## The Bottom Line

Fast travel in *AC Black Flag Resynced* is a straightforward but essential feature that unlocks after completing "This Tyro Captain" in Sequence 3. Understanding all three methods — viewpoints, the world map, and taverns — and knowing when to use each one will save you considerable time and make your time with the game considerably more enjoyable. Get through that early story beat, start syncing viewpoints, and the Caribbean will feel a whole lot smaller.
