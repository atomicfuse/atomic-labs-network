## Step 2: Decide Your Format Before You're Under Pressure

The worst time to figure out whether you want IMAX, Dolby, or standard is when a checkout timer is counting down. Think through it now.

**IMAX:** For a film of this scale — an Avengers team-up with presumably massive set pieces — IMAX is worth it if you have a true laser IMAX near you (the larger, 1.43:1 ratio screens). Seats in the center third of the auditorium, roughly rows 8–12 in a 15-row house, give you the full image without neck strain.

**Dolby Cinema / Premium Large Format:** Dolby's sound mixing tends to be exceptional for action-heavy films. If IMAX isn't nearby or the IMAX near you is a smaller "LieMAX" screen, Dolby is a strong alternative. Look for center seats in the rear third — Dolby theaters are calibrated from behind the audience sweet spot.

**Standard:** Perfectly valid, and often the only way to get a specific showtime that works for your schedule. In a standard auditorium, avoid the front three rows and the extreme side seats. Center, slightly behind the middle row, is your target.

**One practical note:** Premium format allocations are smaller. Those seats will go first. If that's your preference, be ready at the exact minute the sale opens.

## Step 3: Pick Your Showtime Strategically

Opening weekend for a Marvel event film is loud and full of energy — that's part of the fun. But if you want the experience without the chaos:

- **Thursday evening previews** are a sweet spot: enthusiastic crowd, but not the chaotic Friday rush.
- **Saturday morning or early afternoon** screenings tend to have calmer audiences than prime Saturday evening.
- **If you want the opening-night atmosphere,** go Thursday. If you want the cleaner viewing experience, go Saturday morning.

Also check whether your theater of choice offers reserved seating. Most major chains do now, but Alamo Drafthouse has always been reserved — and their in-seat food/drink service and strict no-talking policy make it a genuinely different kind of MCU screening.

## Step 4: Group Bookings Without the Headache

Going with a group of four or more? Here's where people consistently make mistakes:

- **Don't split up the search.** Designate one person to book everyone's seats at once. Trying to coordinate across multiple devices in real time often results in duplicate purchases or seats that don't line up.
- **Verify the seat map together beforehand.** Pull up the theater's seat map on a separate tab, agree on exactly which seats you want (row letter and seat numbers), then have your designated buyer go straight to those when the sale opens.
- **Have a payment method saved and ready.** The extra 90 seconds to type in a card number can cost you the seats during a hot sale.
