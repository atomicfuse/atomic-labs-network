Exciting developments in the gaming world are afoot as the possibility of a new Prototype game emerges. This potential new entry in the popular game series has sent waves of anticipation through the gaming community.

The buzz began when a Prototype fan spotted a new game listing on the ArtStation portfolio of a 3D artist. This discovery stirred up speculation that the artist could be working on a Prototype game. The post has since been removed, suggesting it may have been prematurely revealed.

## Deeper Dive into the Speculation

The artist in question, Ruairidh MacNeill, is a character artist at Dambuster Studios, a subsidiary of Deep Silver. MacNeill's portfolio displayed a project titled 'Prototype 3', which was the source of the speculation. Given his role and the naming of the project, it's not unreasonable to assume that a Prototype sequel could be in the works.

## Mysterious Removal of the Listing

Adding to the intrigue, the listing was swiftly taken down after the news started to circulate. This swift action could indicate that the information was accidentally leaked. Or, it could merely be an unfortunate misunderstanding. In any case, the gaming community is eagerly awaiting an official announcement to clear the air.

## The Legacy of the Prototype Series

Prototype is a beloved video game series that debuted in 2009. Its unique blend of open-world gameplay, high-octane action, and a compelling storyline quickly made it a fan favorite. The second installment, Prototype 2, released in 2012, built on the success of the original, further cementing the series' place in the gaming world.

## A Look into Dambuster Studios

Dambuster Studios, the company that employs the artist who posted the mysterious listing, is known for its work on the Homefront series. As a subsidiary of Deep Silver, it has access to a wealth of resources and talent. This makes the prospect of a new Prototype game under their banner a tantalizing possibility.
