## A Unique Summer Experience in Denali
For travelers seeking adventure and solitude, Denali National Park in Alaska has become a rare gem. Thanks to a landslide in 2021, the Park Road is closed, limiting the usual influx of visitors. This situation creates a perfect storm for travel enthusiasts who want to immerse themselves in nature without the usual crowds. But why should you care? Because 2023 presents a last chance for a truly unique experience in one of America’s most iconic national parks.

## The Impact of Climate Change and Road Closure
The landscape of Denali is no stranger to change. The closure of the Park Road due to climate-related road damage has altered the access to the park for the foreseeable future. Until 2027, Denali's interior—typically bustling with tourists—will be accessible only by specific routes. As climate change continues to impact travel dynamics, Denali serves as a critical case study. This summer, the park's backcountry is quieter than usual, offering an exceptional environment for authentic wilderness experiences.

## How to Explore Denali’s Remote Areas
The 49-mile section of Denali Park Road that’s currently closed has always been a major draw for visitors. Still, those who crave adventure can seize this opportunity to access the park differently. Here are two main options:

### Fly-In Lodges
1. **Kantishna Roadhouse**: Located deep in the wilderness, Kantishna invites visitors to experience modern comforts within stunning natural surroundings. Enjoy guided hikes and exceptional wildlife viewing as you immerse yourself in the Alaskan bush.  
2. **Denali Backcountry Lodge**: With a strong focus on sustainability, this lodge balances luxury with an eco-friendly approach. They offer an especially remote experience and cater to those who are deeply passionate about preserving the environment.
3. **Camp Denali**: Known for its commitment to adventure and education, Camp Denali is the most rugged of the three options. Here, guests can experience the true Alaskan wilderness while enjoying home-cooked meals and an opportunity to connect with fellow adventurers.

Flying into these lodges allows you to skip extensive traveling by car while enjoying the sights from above. This option not only provides a unique way to see Denali, but it also gives you immediate access to the park’s stunning scenery away from larger crowds.

### Backpacking Beyond Mile 43
For the more adventurous souls, backpacking into the park is a striking alternative. As you travel beyond mile 43 (the usual stopping point for vehicles), you’ll find yourself on hiking trails that offer solitude, stunning views, and unparalleled access to Alaska’s diverse wildlife. Picture yourself hiking through meadows filled with wildflowers while searching for bears, wolves, and caribou—all without the hustle and bustle of typical tourist traffic.

## Why Experience Denali This Summer?
There are myriad reasons to prioritize a visit to Denali National Park this summer. Here are just a few:

- **Unique Access**: This is a limited-time opportunity to experience the interior wilderness, providing a unique glimpse of what Denali has to offer when traditional access is restricted.
- **Sustained Nature**: Visiting this summer means you acknowledge the changing climate and support efforts to protect the park during its most vulnerable time.
