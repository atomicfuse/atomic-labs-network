## Lawmakers Target Growing Crypto Political Influence

A new bipartisan proposal in Congress aims to dramatically limit the power of wealthy donors and corporations by capping individual super PAC contributions at $5,000. The Abolish Super PACs Act, introduced in both the House and Senate, comes as crypto-backed political groups have emerged as major players in Washington's policy battles.

According to reports, crypto-linked super PACs have become increasingly influential in fights over stablecoins, market structure, and digital asset regulation. This growing financial muscle in political circles has prompted lawmakers to push for campaign finance reform as election spending is expected to surge in 2026.

## The $5,000 Cap Proposal

The proposed legislation would fundamentally change how super PACs operate by imposing strict donation limits. Currently, these political action committees can accept unlimited contributions from individuals and corporations, allowing for massive concentrated spending on elections and policy advocacy.

The bill represents a direct challenge to the current system that has enabled crypto industry groups to pour significant resources into influencing regulatory outcomes. According to reports, the proposed cap would curb concentrated election spending that has become a hallmark of modern political campaigns.
