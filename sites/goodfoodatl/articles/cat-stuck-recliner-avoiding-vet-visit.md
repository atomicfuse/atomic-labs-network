Sometimes our furry family members go to extraordinary lengths to avoid what's best for them – and one clever cat in Kansas took the concept of "hiding from the vet" to a whole new level.

![Cat rescue from recliner chair](https://cdnph.upi.com/ph/st/th/1141775481405/2026/i/17754815368500/v2.1/Cat-gets-stuck-in-recliner-while-trying-to-avoid-vet-visit.jpg?lg=4)

The Overland Park Police Department recently shared an amusing yet dramatic rescue story on their social media that has pet lovers everywhere both laughing and sympathizing. A resourceful feline, determined to skip its annual veterinary checkup, made what seemed like the perfect escape plan – hiding inside the mechanical workings of a reclining chair.

Unfortunately for our furry escape artist, the plan backfired spectacularly when it became completely stuck within the chair's inner mechanisms.

What started as a routine vet appointment quickly turned into a multi-agency rescue operation. The Overland Park Fire Department had to bring out power tools, using a saw to carefully cut through the chair while animal control officers assisted in the delicate extraction process.

"The feline is fine and arrived to the vet for its annual checkup," the police department confirmed in their social media post, adding that the cat was "desperately trying to escape its owner for a vet visit" when the mishap occurred.

This heartwarming story serves as a gentle reminder to pet parents everywhere: our beloved companions might go to great lengths to avoid necessary care, but with patience, teamwork, and sometimes a little creative problem-solving, we can ensure they get the health attention they need.

For those of us who share our homes and meals with pets, this tale also highlights the important role our animal companions play in our daily lives – even when they're being a bit dramatic about routine healthcare. After all, a healthy pet is a happy dining companion, whether they're begging for table scraps or simply keeping us company in the kitchen.

The rescued cat is reportedly doing well and has likely learned that hiding spots should be thoroughly tested before committing to them – especially when there's a vet appointment on the calendar!
