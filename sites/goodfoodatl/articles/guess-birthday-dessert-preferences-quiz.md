## How Sweet Preferences Connect to Personality

While it might sound far-fetched, food psychologists have long studied the connection between our taste preferences and personality traits. Some researchers suggest that our dessert choices can reflect everything from our risk-taking tendencies to our nostalgic connections with childhood memories.

![Quiz creator profile](https://img.buzzfeed.com/buzzfeed-static/static/user_images/X3gPZZ3BY_large.jpg?crop=493%3A493%3B0%2C0&downsize=120:*&output-format=jpg&output-quality=auto)

The premise behind this quiz is simple yet intriguing: by selecting your favorite desserts from each letter of the alphabet, we'll analyze patterns that might correlate with birth month characteristics, seasonal preferences, and even astrological tendencies that influence our sweet tooth.

## Why Desserts Starting with 'S' Dominate

You've probably noticed that many beloved desserts start with the letter 'S' – strawberry shortcake, sundaes, soufflés, strudels, and s'mores, just to name a few. This isn't coincidence; it's likely due to the satisfying sound of the letter 'S' combined with the sweet sensations these treats provide.

![Community team approval](https://img.buzzfeed.com/buzzfeed-static/static/store-an-image-prod-us-east-1/p9aVli648.png?downsize=120:*&output-format=jpg&output-quality=auto)

Linguists call this phenomenon "sound symbolism," where certain sounds are associated with specific experiences. The 'S' sound often evokes smoothness and sweetness, making it a natural fit for dessert names.

## Ready to Take the Challenge?

This quiz works by having you select desserts for each letter of the alphabet, then analyzing your choices through a complex algorithm that considers factors like:

- Seasonal ingredients in your preferred treats
- Complexity levels of your chosen desserts
- Cultural origins of your selections
- Texture preferences (creamy vs. crunchy, etc.)
- Temperature preferences (hot vs. cold desserts)

## The Science Behind Food Preferences

Our dessert preferences are shaped by numerous factors including childhood experiences, cultural background, seasonal availability during our formative years, and even our birth month's typical weather patterns. People born in winter months, for instance, often gravitate toward warming, comfort desserts, while summer babies might prefer lighter, fruitier options.
