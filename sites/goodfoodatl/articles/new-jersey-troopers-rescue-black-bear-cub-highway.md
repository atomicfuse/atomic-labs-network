In an unexpected turn of events that highlights our connection to wildlife and nature, New Jersey State Police troopers became unlikely heroes when they rescued a tiny black bear cub found abandoned alongside a busy highway.

![Black bear cub rescued from ditch next to New Jersey highway](https://cdnph.upi.com/ph/st/th/7771775482024/2026/i/17754821301389/v2.1/Black-bear-cub-rescued-from-ditch-next-to-New-Jersey-highway.jpg?lg=4)

The heartwarming rescue unfolded when personnel from Troop B Perryville Station received what they described as an "unusual call" regarding a solitary bear cub spotted in a roadside ditch. The location was particularly concerning - Interstate 78 East at milepost 12.2 in Union Township, where heavy traffic could have posed serious dangers to the vulnerable young animal.

When troopers arrived at the scene, they discovered the small cub completely alone and clearly in need of immediate help. The officers acted quickly to ensure both the animal's safety and that of passing motorists. "He was safely secured, transported back to the barracks, and later released to the Department of Environmental Protection for proper care," the State Police shared on their social media channels.

This touching rescue reminds us of the delicate balance between human development and wildlife habitats. As food enthusiasts who appreciate the bounty that nature provides, we often forget that the same ecosystems that give us wild mushrooms, berries, and game also serve as homes for creatures like this little bear.

The cub's story also underscores the importance of wildlife conservation efforts. Black bears, while native to New Jersey, face challenges as their natural habitats intersect with human development. Fortunately, the Department of Environmental Protection has protocols in place to care for orphaned or abandoned wildlife, giving this young bear the best chance at survival.

While we may never know exactly how this cub ended up alone by the highway - whether orphaned or separated from its mother - the quick action of the troopers ensured a positive outcome. The rescue serves as a beautiful example of humans stepping in to protect wildlife when needed, maintaining the natural balance that ultimately supports the food systems we all depend on.
