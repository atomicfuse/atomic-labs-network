## What Are 'Super-Puff' Planets?
Imagine a planet so light that it’s denser than candy floss! Welcome to the realm of 'super-puff' planets, a term that defines these extraordinary celestial bodies with densities that leave scientists wide-eyed in disbelief. The recent discovery of two super-puff exoplanets, TOI-791 b and TOI-791 c, has rocked the astronomical community, revealing new insights into how planetary atmospheres can evolve in the depths of space.

## The Discovery
An international team of astronomers, led by the University of Liège, employed a blend of advanced tools and citizen science to identify these astonishing planets. Utilizing data from NASA's Transiting Exoplanet Survey Satellite (TESS), combined with observational data from Antarctic telescopes, the team pieced together the puzzle surrounding these exotic worlds.

### The Lightness of Being
These super-puff planets are among the lowest density giant planets ever discovered. The duo's densities are so incredibly light that if you were to scoop a bit of them up, they would practically float—imagine the visual spectacle! The cores of these planets are likely composed of gas mixes, mostly hydrogen and helium. This pulsating atmosphere makes them unique laboratory specimens in the search for understanding planetary formation.
