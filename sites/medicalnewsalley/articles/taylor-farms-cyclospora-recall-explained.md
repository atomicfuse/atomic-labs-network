### Why Does a False Positive Matter?

A false positive doesn't indicate that the product is safe. In food safety, this term refers to a test result that suggests a product is contaminated when it isn't. Although the FDA's testing might have been incorrect, that doesn't eliminate the necessity for the recall. The majority of food recalls are driven by traceback evidence instead of definitive lab results. 

Investigators use what’s called traceback investigations. This involves tracing the food product back through the supply chain to identify the point of contamination. In this case, the ongoing investigation is still linked to other evidence, leading to the decision to keep the Taylor Farms products on recall.

### The Challenge of Testing for Cyclospora

Testing for cyclospora is notoriously tricky. The parasite can be present in low levels and may not always be detected in food samples, making definitive results elusive. Even if a test comes back negative, it doesn't mean that a product is safe.
