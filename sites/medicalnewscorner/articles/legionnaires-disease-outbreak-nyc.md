## What Is Legionnaires’ Disease?
Legionnaires’ disease is a severe form of pneumonia caused by the bacterium *Legionella*. Typically, it is contracted by inhaling water droplets containing these bacteria, which can be found in sources like cooling towers, hot tubs, and large plumbing systems. It does not spread from person to person.

## Symptoms to Watch For
Knowing the symptoms of Legionnaires’ disease can ensure early detection and treatment. Key symptoms include:
- **Cough**: This may be dry or produce phlegm.
- **Shortness of breath**: Difficulty in breathing can worsen over time.
- **Fever**: Patients often experience high fever, chills, and sweating.
- **Muscle aches**: These may accompany fatigue and malaise.

Anyone experiencing these symptoms should consider seeking medical attention, especially if they have recently been in an area with a reported outbreak.
