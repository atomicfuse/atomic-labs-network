## A Medical Milestone in Michigan

Briana Dery, a 33-year-old woman from Twining, Michigan, has made medical history by becoming the first person in the state to receive a combined intestinal and kidney transplant in a single operation. After nearly two decades of life-threatening illness and organ failure, Dery is now looking ahead to a healthy future following the groundbreaking procedure.

## The Rarity of the Procedure

Combined intestinal-kidney transplants are extraordinarily rare in the United States, with only 56 such procedures ever performed nationwide. This statistic underscores the exceptional nature of Dery's case and represents a major regional medical milestone for Michigan's transplant community.

The procedure reflects how transplant medicine is evolving to expand treatment options for patients who were once considered too complex or high-risk for surgical intervention.

## An 18-Year Medical Journey

Dery's path to this historic transplant began 18 years ago with the onset of life-threatening illness. According to reports, she endured nearly two decades of repeated complications and progressive organ failure before becoming a candidate for the combined transplant procedure.

Her case highlights the challenging medical journey that many patients with complex organ failure face, often requiring years of treatment before advanced surgical options become available.

## The "Forbidden Organ" Challenge

The intestine has historically been referred to as the "forbidden organ" in transplant medicine due to the extreme complexity of the procedure. Unlike other organ transplants, intestinal transplantation presents unique surgical and medical challenges that have limited its widespread adoption.

The complexity of transplanting intestinal tissue, combined with the need for simultaneous kidney transplantation, required exceptional coordination and expertise from the medical team.

## Coordinated 10-Hour Surgery

The Henry Ford Health team successfully completed the complex 10-hour dual-organ transplant procedure. According to reports, this operation required extensive coordination among multiple surgical specialists and support staff to manage the intricacies of transplanting two different organs simultaneously.

The length and complexity of the procedure demonstrate the technical challenges involved in combined organ transplantation and the level of expertise required to perform such operations safely.
