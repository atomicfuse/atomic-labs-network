## Free Access to Canada's Natural Wonders

Canada is making its stunning natural landscapes more accessible this summer by waiving admission fees at all Parks Canada sites from June 19 through September 7. According to reports, this unprecedented offer covers more than 40 national parks, 171 historic sites, and 5 marine conservation areas across the country.

The initiative comes at a time when travel costs are at peak levels, making this free-access window a rare opportunity for budget-conscious travelers. In addition to waived entrance fees, visitors can take advantage of 25% discounts on camping throughout the promotion period.

## Must-Visit Destinations During Free Access

While all Parks Canada sites will be accessible without entrance fees, several standout destinations are highlighted as must-visits during this promotional period. Banff National Park, one of Canada's most iconic destinations, offers world-class hiking and mountain scenery in the Canadian Rockies.

Pacific Rim National Park Reserve provides coastal adventures with opportunities for surfing, storm watching, and exploring temperate rainforests along British Columbia's rugged coastline. For those interested in historical significance, the Viking settlement at L'Anse aux Meadows in Newfoundland offers a unique glimpse into North America's earliest European contact.

## Adventure Opportunities Across Canada

The free access initiative opens doors to diverse adventure activities across Canada's protected areas. Visitors can explore hiking trails ranging from gentle nature walks to challenging mountain ascents. Marine conservation areas offer kayaking and diving opportunities, while historic sites provide cultural tourism experiences.

According to reports, the program encompasses various adventure types including hiking, kayaking, diving, and literary tourism, making it appealing to travelers with different interests and activity levels.
