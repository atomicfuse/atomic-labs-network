## Understanding Bison Behavior
Bison are powerful animals and can weigh up to 2,000 pounds. They generally prefer to avoid human interaction, but if they feel threatened, they can charge. Understanding their behavior is key to ensuring your safety:
- **Aggression is a Reaction:** Bison typically flee from perceived threats, but if approached, they might stand their ground or charge.
- **Protective Mothers:** Female bison are particularly protective of their young calves. If you see a calf, it's best to keep your distance and not provoke the mother.
- **Signs of Stress:** If a bison raises its tail, snorts, or stomps its feet, it’s a clear indication you’re too close.

## Safety Guidelines for Tourists
### 1. Maintain a Safe Distance
Always stay at least 25 yards (about 23 meters) away from bison. If you encounter a group, back away slowly and avoid any sudden movements. Maintain your distance to allow these wild animals their space. 

### 2. Stay on Designated Paths
Stick to marked trails and boardwalks. Wildlife in Yellowstone is accustomed to humans staying on these paths, which reduces chances of unexpected encounters.

### 3. Use Binoculars or a Zoom Lens
For a closer view of bison, use binoculars or a camera with a zoom lens. This allows you to enjoy watching these majestic creatures without risking your safety.

### 4. Educate Yourself and Fellow Travelers
Before your trip, familiarize yourself and your fellow travelers with wildlife safety rules. Share these tips with others, especially children who may not fully understand the concept of distance.
