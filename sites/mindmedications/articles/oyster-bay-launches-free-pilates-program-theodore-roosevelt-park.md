## Free Saturday Morning Pilates Coming to Theodore Roosevelt Park

Oyster Bay residents will soon have access to a new outdoor wellness opportunity as Town Supervisor Joseph Saladino announced the upcoming registration for the Town's Pilates in the Park program. The 10-week fitness series is designed to bring community members together for accessible outdoor exercise in a beautiful park setting.

## Program Details and Schedule

The Pilates in the Park program will take place every Saturday at Theodore Roosevelt Park, beginning July 11th. According to the announcement, classes will run from 10:00 to 11:00 a.m., providing participants with a perfect way to start their weekend with mindful movement and fresh air.

The program represents part of the Town's broader summer community programming efforts, as local officials work to provide residents with accessible wellness activities that combine fitness benefits with outdoor enjoyment and community connection.

## Why Pilates Works for Everyone

Pilates offers unique advantages as an outdoor fitness option, particularly for community programs that aim to serve diverse age groups and fitness levels. As a low-impact workout method, Pilates focuses on core strength, flexibility, and body awareness while being gentle on joints and suitable for beginners through advanced practitioners.

The outdoor setting at Theodore Roosevelt Park adds additional wellness benefits beyond the exercise itself. Practicing Pilates in nature can enhance the mind-body connection that's central to this form of movement, while fresh air and natural surroundings contribute to stress reduction and mental clarity.

## Building Community Through Wellness

Community-based fitness programs like Pilates in the Park serve multiple purposes beyond individual health benefits. The timing of the Pilates program creates opportunities for participants to extend their wellness experience at Theodore Roosevelt Park. Arriving early for a brief walk or meditation before class can help center the mind and prepare the body for movement. After class, the park setting provides space for additional activities like walking, stretching, or simply enjoying time in nature.
