## AMC's Vampire Series Sets Sights on Anne Rice's Most Controversial Material

AMC's *Interview With the Vampire* is preparing to venture into the strangest corners of Anne Rice's vampire mythology, with showrunner Rolin Jones already eyeing the author's most controversial later works for future seasons.

According to reports, while season 3 will introduce the ancient vampire queen Akasha, Jones has his sights set on adapting even wilder material from Rice's extensive vampire chronicles, including *Memnoch the Devil* — the book where Lestat literally drinks Jesus Christ's blood.

## Embracing the "Psychotic, Weird, Wild" Elements

Jones has made it clear that the series won't shy away from Rice's most outlandish concepts. "If you didn't grab one or two of the totally psychotic, weird, wild things, you'd be missing out on what makes her as an artist, singular," according to reports.

This approach represents a bold creative choice as the series expands beyond its original premise. The show is reportedly positioning itself to explore glam rock elements, ancient vampire mythology, and religious horror — territory that could make it one of television's weirdest prestige genre adaptations.

## What's Coming in Season 3 and Beyond

Season 3 will reportedly introduce Akasha, a character whose arrival changes everything in Rice's vampire universe. However, the showrunner's ambitions extend far beyond this pivotal addition to the cast.

The series appears to be building toward adapting some of Rice's most controversial religious content, including the infamous scene where Lestat encounters and drinks the blood of Jesus Christ in *Memnoch the Devil*. This material represents some of the most challenging and divisive content in Rice's vampire chronicles.

## Rice's Catholic Influence on the Vampire Mythology

Anne Rice's Catholic background heavily influenced her vampire novels, creating a unique blend of religious themes and supernatural horror. According to reports, this religious influence will play a significant role in the show's future direction.

The author's exploration of Christian mythology through the lens of vampire fiction created some of the most unique — and controversial — moments in the genre. These elements distinguish Rice's work from other vampire fiction and represent the "singular" artistic vision that Jones appears determined to honor.
