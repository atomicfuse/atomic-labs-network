Prime Video's latest book-to-screen romance adaptation is generating serious streaming buzz. **Every Year After**, based on Carley Fortune's bestselling novel *Every Summer After*, brings a beloved second-chance love story to life — complete with dual timelines, a nostalgic lakeside setting, and a cast assembled to carry both the past and present versions of its central romance.

For fans of the BookTok-favorite novel already deep in their feelings, and for newcomers discovering it through the show, here's your complete guide to the cast of *Every Year After* and the characters they play.

## Sadie Soverall and Juliette Hawk as Percy

Persephone "Percy" Fraser sits at the heart of the story, and the show uses two actors to tell her journey across timelines. **Sadie Soverall** steps into the present-day version of Percy, while **Juliette Hawk** plays young Percy in the flashback sequences. The dual-casting approach allows the series to fully explore how Percy's summers at Barry's Bay shaped the woman she becomes — and why Sam Florek never quite left her orbit.

## Matt Cornett and Blue Clarke as Sam Florek

Opposite Percy is Sam Florek, the other half of this emotionally messy second-chance romance. **Matt Cornett** takes on adult Sam, with **Blue Clarke** portraying the younger version of the character in the past timeline. Sam is very much the kind of role that requires both charm and heartbreak in equal measure, and the series leans into the contrast between who he was and who he's become.

## Michael Bradway and Carson MacCormac as Charlie Florek

Sam's brother Charlie is played by **Michael Bradway** in the present-day storyline, with **Carson MacCormac** handling the younger version of the character. Charlie is part of the tight-knit Barry's Bay circle that gives the story much of its warmth and complication, and the Florek family dynamic is central to understanding Sam's emotional arc.

## Aurora Perrineau as Chantal

**Aurora Perrineau** joins the ensemble as Chantal, adding another familiar face to what is shaping up to be a well-assembled cast. Perrineau brings experience from previous screen work that viewers may recognize, making her a strong addition to the Barry's Bay friend group.

## Abigail Cowen as Delilah

**Abigail Cowen**, perhaps one of the more widely recognized names in the cast, plays Delilah. Cowen has built a following through her previous television work, and her presence in *Every Year After* is likely to draw in viewers who've followed her career.

## Joseph Chiu as Jordie

**Joseph Chiu** rounds out the Barry's Bay circle as Jordie. Like the other supporting players, Jordie contributes to the ensemble texture that makes second-chance romance stories work — these aren't just two people reconnecting in a vacuum, but characters shaped by shared history and community.

## Elisha Cuthbert as Sue Florek

Veteran actress **Elisha Cuthbert** takes on the role of Sue Florek, Sam and Charlie's mother. Cuthbert is one of the most recognizable names attached to the project, and her casting signals the kind of production investment that book adaptations with built-in fanbases tend to attract.

## Why This Adaptation Has Fans Paying Attention

Book-to-screen romance adaptations have become a reliable streaming draw, particularly when the source material carries a passionate readership built on platforms like BookTok. Carley Fortune's novel has that kind of following, and *Every Year After* arrives at a moment when audiences are clearly hungry for nostalgic, emotionally resonant love stories told across time.

The dual-timeline structure — cutting between the summers that defined Percy and Sam's relationship and the present-day reckoning with what was left unresolved — is one of the novel's most beloved features, and the casting of separate actors for past and present versions of the central characters suggests the show is committing to that structure rather than softening it.

## What to Watch For

For viewers coming in cold, the Barry's Bay setting functions almost as a character in itself — a place where summers meant something, and where old feelings have a way of resurfacing. The ensemble around Percy and Sam, from the Florek family to the wider friend group, is what gives the central romance its stakes and its complications.

With *Every Year After* now on Prime Video, fans of the novel finally get to see how Fortune's story translates to screen — and whether this cast can deliver the emotional payoff readers have been anticipating.
