Rhaenyra's biggest hurdle comes from her own indecision. While she believes in a compassionate approach to leadership, this idealism stands at odds with the harsh realities of ruling during a civil war. Her mercy, rather than being a strength, becomes a potential fatal flaw, allowing threats to fester unchecked. Her reluctance to make hard choices—whether to execute potential traitors or reward those loyal to her—illustrates a disconnect between her ideals and the ruthless world of power.

An especially telling moment occurs when the High Septon refuses to anoint Rhaenyra, emphasizing a critical misunderstanding among the Targaryens regarding power and faith. This refusal echoes her faltering authority, signaling that a queen's rightful claim isn’t simply borne out of blood; it requires the steadfast backing of the faith and her people.

## The Clash of Pragmatism vs. Morality
