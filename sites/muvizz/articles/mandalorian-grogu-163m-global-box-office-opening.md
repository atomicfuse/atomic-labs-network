## Star Wars Returns to Theaters in Force

*Star Wars: The Mandalorian and Grogu* has made a commanding entrance at the global box office, capturing $163 million worldwide in its opening weekend according to reports. The film's strong performance marks a significant test for Disney as the studio attempts to translate a popular Disney+ series into theatrical success.

The weekend box office results demonstrate the continuing power of established franchises, with multiple sequels and franchise entries dominating the top positions globally.

## Michael Biopic Approaches Historic Milestone

The Michael Jackson biopic *Michael* continued its impressive run, earning $55.1 million worldwide over the weekend and bringing its global total to $789.2 million according to reports. This performance puts the film within striking distance of the $800 million mark, positioning it among the most successful music biopics in recent memory.

The film's sustained box office performance highlights the global appeal of music-centered storytelling and biographical content in theaters.

## International Hits Round Out Top Performers

*Dear You*, a Chinese local production, claimed the third position with $39.5 million worldwide for the weekend, accumulating $151.5 million in total earnings. The film's success demonstrates that locally-focused stories can achieve significant commercial success within domestic markets.

*The Devil Wears Prada 2* maintained strong performance with $37.7 million worldwide over the weekend, pushing its global total to $608 million according to reports. The sequel's continued success reflects the enduring appeal of nostalgic franchise revivals.

## Horror and Thriller Genres Show Strength

*Obsession* rounded out the top five with $39.2 million worldwide for the weekend and $81.9 million total, while *The Sheep Detectives* earned $18.4 million worldwide over the weekend with $86 million total. *Passenger* also maintained presence in the top performers with $15.3 million worldwide for the weekend.

## Summer Box Office Trends Emerge

According to reports, the summer box office is accelerating with franchise films, music biopics, horror productions, and Chinese local hits all competing for global audience attention. The diverse range of successful content suggests that multiple genres and storytelling approaches are finding commercial viability in the current theatrical landscape.
