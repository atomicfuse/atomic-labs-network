## The Delight of Moong Dal Pakode
Is there anything better than enjoying golden, crispy moong dal pakode with a steaming cup of chai? Known for their delightful crunch and savory flavor, pakode can quickly become the star of any snacking occasion—especially on rainy days. But what’s the secret behind achieving that street-style crunch at home? MasterChef Pankaj Bhadouria has shared five essential tips that can make all the difference.

### 1. The Right Batter: Avoid Water and Whisk Well
A well-prepared batter is the foundation of any good pakoda. Chef Pankaj emphasizes skipping the water while preparing the moong dal batter. Instead, whipping the dal until creamy ensures a fluffy texture. This aeration process helps your pakode rise, making them lighter and crispier.

### 2. Use Rice Flour for Extra Crunch
Adding rice flour to your batter can turn your good pakode into great ones! This ingredient is a game-changer, offering that additional crispiness. When mixed with the moong dal, rice flour creates the perfect texture that makes each bite irresistible. A ratio of about 1:4 (rice flour to moong dal) works well to keep the pakode crunchy.

### 3. Don’t Forget the Baking Soda
A pinch of baking soda can work wonders in achieving that puffed-up, airy quality. When introduced to the batter, baking soda reacts and helps create small bubbles, which makes the pakode light. However, don’t overdo it; a little goes a long way!

### 4. Hot Oil is Key
One of Chef Pankaj’s golden rules is to fry your pakode in hot oil. If the oil isn't hot enough, your pakode will absorb more oil, leaving them greasy and soggy instead of crispy. Aim for an oil temperature between 350°F to 375°F (175°C to 190°C). If you're unsure, drop a small piece of batter into the oil; if it sizzles immediately, you're ready!

### 5. The Temperature Check
As simple as it sounds, monitoring your frying temperature can make or break the crispiness of your pakode. Too low, and the pakode soak up oil; too high, and they burn before cooking through. Use a thermometer to keep the heat consistent, ensuring efficiently cooked, beautifully crisp pakode every time.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid
While preparing pakode, here are a few common mistakes to keep in mind that could ruin your quest for the perfect crunch:
- **Too Much Water:** Adding excess water makes your pakoda batter runny.
- **Insufficient Heating Time:** Not letting your oil reach the optimal temperature.
- **Holding for Too Long:** If left to sit, your batter can settle, impacting the texture upon frying.
