## The Science Behind Chilling Red Wine

Sommeliers and winemakers featured in the guide explain that cooling specific red wines enhances their fruit and freshness, making them more approachable for summer drinking. The practice isn't just trendy—it's scientifically sound, with temperature playing a crucial role in how we perceive flavor profiles.

The guide identifies five key varietals that shine when chilled: pinot noir, gamay, frappato, grenache, and cinsault. These lighter-bodied reds transform when served at cooler temperatures, offering a refreshing alternative to traditional warm-temperature service.
