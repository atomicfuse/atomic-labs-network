## The Protein Gap in Traditional Indian Meals

A fitness coach has released a comprehensive protein cheat sheet specifically designed for busy Indian women, addressing a common nutritional gap in traditional healthy meals. According to reports, while staples like dal-roti-sabzi, poha, dosa, and khichdi are nutritious, they often fall short of providing adequate protein on their own.

The guide aims to help women reach approximately 90-120 grams of protein daily through practical "protein anchors" that can be easily incorporated into familiar Indian cuisine.

## Essential Protein Sources for Indian Women

The cheat sheet highlights 15 key protein combinations that busy women can rely on:

**Animal-Based Options:**
- 4 whole eggs provide 24g of protein
- 200g egg whites deliver 22g
- 100g chicken breast contains 25-30g
- 100g fish offers 22-25g
- 100g prawns supply 20-24g

**Vegetarian Protein Powerhouses:**
- 100g paneer contains 18-20g
- 150g tofu provides 18-22g
- 200g Greek yogurt or hung curd offers 18-22g
- One scoop of whey or plant protein delivers 22-25g
- One cup of cooked chana, rajma, or dal provides 12-18g
- 50g dry soya chunks contain over 25g
- 100g tempeh offers 18-20g

## Smart Meal Combinations

The guide also features practical meal combinations that maximize protein content:

- One bowl of curd plus two boiled eggs equals 25g of protein
- Two besan chillas with curd provide 22-28g
- Paneer or tofu bhurji with one roti delivers over 25g
