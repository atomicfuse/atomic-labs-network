## A Pioneering Festival Comes to an End

This summer marks the end of an era for the wine community as Queer Wine Fest, the world's first LGBTQ-focused wine festival, holds its final celebration on June 28, 2026, in McMinnville, Oregon's Willamette Valley. After five successful years, founder Remy Drabkin has decided to conclude the groundbreaking event, citing the achievement of its original mission to uplift the queer wine community and support LGBTQ vintners.

## From Humble Beginnings to Cultural Impact

The festival's journey reflects remarkable growth and influence within the wine industry. According to reports, what began as an intimate gathering of 100 attendees has expanded to welcome over 300 participants, creating a significant cultural moment that extends far beyond its Oregon origins.

Drabkin's decision to end the festival comes not from failure, but from success. The event has successfully shifted representation in the wine industry and catalyzed systemic change in wine culture and LGBTQ entrepreneurship, achieving the very goals it set out to accomplish.
