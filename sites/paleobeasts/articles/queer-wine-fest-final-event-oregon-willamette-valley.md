## A Farewell to a Pioneering Wine Celebration

The world's first LGBTQ-focused wine festival is preparing to pour its final glasses. Queer Wine Fest, which has been celebrating queer wine culture in Oregon's Willamette Valley for five years, will hold its concluding event on June 28, 2026, in McMinnville.

Founder Remy Drabkin has announced the decision to end the festival after achieving its original mission to uplift the queer wine community and support LGBTQ vintners. According to reports, the festival has successfully completed what it set out to accomplish, making this summer's event a meaningful finale rather than an ending born of struggle.

These practices reflected founder Remy Drabkin's attention to detail and commitment to creating meaningful experiences rather than simply scaling up. The festival's approach emphasized quality relationships and environmental responsibility over maximizing attendance or profit.
