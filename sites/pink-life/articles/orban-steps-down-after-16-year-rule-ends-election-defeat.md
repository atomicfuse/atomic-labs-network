Viktor Orbán, the strongman who dominated Hungarian politics for over a decade and a half, has stepped down from parliament following a crushing electoral defeat that marks a potential turning point for democracy in Europe.

"Our task now is not in parliament," Orbán stated, signaling his departure from the legislative body while vowing a radical "reorganization" of his political camp. The resignation comes two weeks after the election that ended his long grip on power.
