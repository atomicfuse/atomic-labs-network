## How This AI Tool Works
Researchers at the IMDEA Materials Institute have pioneered an innovative AI model that predicts the flame resistance of epoxy resins. This forward-thinking approach utilizes data derived from phosphorus-based flame-retardant composites. By analyzing patterns and properties within this data, the AI can forecast crucial fire-safety indicators such as UL-94 and LOI (Limited Oxygen Index). 

### What Are UL-94 and LOI?
For many, the acronyms UL-94 and LOI may sound technical, but they hold significant importance in fire safety. 
- **UL-94** refers to a standard that assesses the flammability of plastic materials. It categorizes materials into different levels based on how they behave when exposed to fire.
- **LOI** measures the minimum concentration of oxygen required for combustion. A higher LOI indicates that a material is more flame-resistant, meaning less likely to catch fire in low-oxygen environments.

With these metrics, engineers can more quickly determine a material's suitability and safety in various applications — from EV batteries to building materials.

## Why Are Flame-Retardant Materials So Difficult to Develop?
Developing effective flame-retardant materials isn't a walk in the park. The challenge lies in juggling performance, cost, and environmental considerations. Many traditional flame retardants contain halogens, which are effective but pose environmental and health hazards. As a result, there is a significant push toward halogen-free alternatives that still adhere to safety regulations while being environmentally friendly. This AI tool helps streamline the search for such materials, providing insights into the best combinations and formulations.
