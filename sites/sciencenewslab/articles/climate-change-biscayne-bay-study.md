## The Impact of Climate Change on Biscayne Bay
If you’ve ever gazed at the sparkling waters of Biscayne Bay, you may have felt a sense of tranquility. But beneath that beauty, significant changes are underway. A major study spanning two decades has provided alarming insights into how climate change is reshaping this crucial marine ecosystem in South Florida. Researchers have been monitoring various water quality factors since 2001, and what they’ve found could call into question the future of the bay's delicate balance.

## The Big Findings: Warmer, Saltier, and More Acidic
Researchers analyzed data collected from 34 monitoring stations around Biscayne Bay, documenting changes in temperature, salinity, dissolved oxygen, and pH levels. The findings show that the bay's waters are becoming:

### Warmer
Increased temperatures can have a cascading effect on marine life. Warmer waters can lead to coral bleaching, disrupt the food chain, and encourage the proliferation of harmful algal blooms. With every passing summer, Biscayne Bay's creatures are adjusting to these new, heat-stressed conditions.

### Saltier
With sea level rise thanks to climate change, seawater is making its way into freshwater aquifers, increasing salinity levels. This saltwater intrusion threatens the delicate balance that supports both marine and freshwater ecosystems. For a region relying heavily on freshwater for drinking and agriculture, this shift poses serious challenges.

### More Acidic
Ocean acidification is no longer a theoretical issue; it's a reality affecting Biscayne Bay. Changes in pH levels impact everything from seagrass meadows to the tiny creatures that underpin the marine food web. This acidity affects shellfish and can lead to reduced biodiversity, which is critical for a healthy ecosystem.
