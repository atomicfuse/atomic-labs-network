## What Are Gravitational Waves?
Gravitational waves are ripples in the fabric of spacetime, produced by some of the universe's most violent events, like merging black holes. Think of them as the sound of the cosmos, an almost inaudible hum that speaks of colossal phenomena occurring light-years away. Just as a pebble thrown into a pond creates ripples, these waves signal the movements and fusions of celestial bodies, allowing scientists to 'hear' the universe's most secretive corners, including black holes.

## Understanding the Event Horizon 
To grasp the significance of this discovery, we first need to understand what an event horizon is. Imagine the point of no return when crossing a waterfall—once you go over, there's no coming back. The event horizon defines the boundary surrounding a black hole; once anything crosses it, not even light can escape, sealing its fate forever. It’s a one-way ticket to oblivion, shrouded in mystery until now.
