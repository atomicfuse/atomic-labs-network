One of the significant challenges in the concept of giant mirrors is the phenomenon of radiation pressure. This concept explains how sunlight exerts a force on objects in space. While this light-push helps propel solar sails, it can also destabilize large reflective structures if not carefully managed. Scientists have been exploring the dynamics of these mirrors to understand how they can be kept in orbit without succumbing to solar radiation.

### The Impact of Orbital Configuration
The latest findings suggest that the stability of these massive mirrors isn't just a matter of technology and materials but also heavily dependent on their orbital configuration. #### Retrograde and Close Orbits
Interestingly, orbits that are close to their planets or retrograde (moving in the opposite direction of a planet's rotation) appear to be more stable.
