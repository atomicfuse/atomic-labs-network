According to reports, this engineering breakthrough represents the successful transition from construction to astronomical testing, validating the design for what will become a transformative 244-antenna array.

The prototype has demonstrated its ability to make independent observations while seamlessly collaborating with the existing NSF Very Large Array in New Mexico. This integration capability proves that the new technology can work as a unified system with the current 27-antenna configuration—a critical engineering requirement for the ambitious project.

The ngVLA promises to deliver unprecedented sensitivity and resolution capabilities.
