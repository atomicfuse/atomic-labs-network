## What Is the Air Quality Index (AQI)?

The Air Quality Index (AQI) is a valuable tool for gauging air pollution levels. It ranges from 0 to 500, providing a quick understanding of health impacts based on current air conditions. Here are the general categories of AQI:
- **0-50:** Good (green)
- **51-100:** Moderate (yellow)
- **101-150:** Unhealthy for sensitive groups (orange)
- **151-200:** Unhealthy (red)
- **201-300:** Very Unhealthy (purple)
- **301-500:** Hazardous (maroon)

With the current wildfire smoke affecting the air, you may see higher AQI levels categorized as unhealthy.

## Checking Local Air Quality

Staying informed about local air quality is crucial during wildfire smoke events. Here are a few resources you can use:
- **AirNow:** This website provides real-time AQI readings for your location. You can enter your zip code to receive specific air quality data.
- **Local Health Departments:** Many local health departments monitor air quality and provide updates on smoke conditions.
- **Weather Apps:** Some weather applications offer AQI readings, making it convenient to check air quality alongside typical weather forecasts.

## When to Limit Outdoor Activities

During high AQI days, especially when it reaches the unhealthy levels (151+), it’s wise to limit outdoor activities. Here are some practical steps:
- **Stay Indoors:** Keep windows closed to minimize smoke entering your home.
- **Avoid Strenuous Exercise:** Outdoor exercise can significantly elevate your inhalation of harmful particles, so opt for indoor workouts when possible.
- **Use HEPA Filters:** If you have an air purifier, running it can help reduce indoor particle levels.

## Simple Indoor Air Steps

To maintain good indoor air quality during wildfire smoke days:
- **Ventilation:** If conditions improve, ventilate your home to circulate fresh air. However, during heavy smoke, keep windows and doors shut.
- **Monitor the AQI:** Use the resources mentioned to keep a close watch on changing air quality. 
- **Use Air Conditioning:** If you have AC, running it can help filter out smoke particles.

## Additional Health Tips

If you or your family members are particularly sensitive to smoke—such as those with asthma or other respiratory conditions—consider the following:
- **Stay informed about your health:** Keep in touch with a healthcare provider if smoke is expected to be pervasive in your area.
- **Have a plan:** Understand the action steps to take if air quality worsens or health issues arise.
