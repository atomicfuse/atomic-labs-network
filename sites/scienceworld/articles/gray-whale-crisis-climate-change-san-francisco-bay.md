## A Marine Ecological Emergency Unfolds

San Francisco Bay is witnessing an unprecedented ecological crisis as gray whales die at record rates, with their population plummeting from 27,000 to just 12,500 over nine years. The primary driver behind this marine tragedy appears to be climate-driven food scarcity. According to reports, changing ocean conditions are disrupting the whales' traditional feeding grounds and migration patterns. Gray whales, which undertake one of the longest migrations in the animal kingdom, are finding their carefully timed journeys increasingly mismatched with food availability.

These shifting migration patterns are bringing both rare research opportunities and new mysteries for marine scientists.
