Everton's team selection will depend on the fitness of several important players. The core of the side is likely to remain built around defensive reliability, with Jordan Pickford expected to play a crucial role in goal. His shot-stopping and command of the box could be vital against City's fluid attacking movement.

## Manchester City team news and tactical options

Manchester City continue to manage fitness issues across the squad. Rodri's absence has been one of the defining factors of their season, reducing the control and defensive balance that he usually provides at the base of midfield. Without him, City have had to find different ways to protect transitions.

Erling Haaland's availability is another major talking point. When fit, he changes the geometry of City's attack by pinning defenders and attacking space behind the back line. If he is unavailable or not ready to start, Guardiola could lean on a more flexible forward line.

Defensively, Ruben Dias and Josko Gvardiol are key figures. City's back line must handle Everton's aerial threat and second balls. That will be especially important at Goodison Park, where the crowd can lift the home side during spells of pressure.

## Tactical battle at Goodison Park

Everton are unlikely to chase possession for long spells. Instead, they will aim to stay compact, close central passing lanes, and force City wide. That approach can frustrate elite opponents, especially if the home side defend their box with authority.

City will try to stretch Everton with width before attacking central spaces. Guardiola's teams often use patient build-up to move defenders out of position. Quick switches of play, underlapping runs, and cut-backs from the byline could be central to their plan.

The first goal will shape the match. If City score early, Everton may be forced to open up. If Everton keep the game level, Goodison Park will grow louder, and the pressure on City will increase.
