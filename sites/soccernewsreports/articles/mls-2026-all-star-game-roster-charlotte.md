## MLS Drops Full 46-Player All-Star Roster Ahead of July 29 Showdown

Major League Soccer has officially announced the complete 46-player roster for the 2026 MLS All-Star Game presented by Chime, bringing together World Cup participants, seasoned veterans, and emerging young talent for what promises to be a landmark occasion. The MLS All-Stars will take on LIGA MX All-Stars on **July 29 in Charlotte, North Carolina**, in a game that also marks the league's **30th anniversary**.

The roster blends global star power with homegrown storylines, and it arrives with significant buzz following the conclusion of the 2026 FIFA World Cup, with no fewer than 13 players on the list coming fresh off World Cup rosters.

## World Cup Stars Headline a Star-Studded Selection

Among the most high-profile names selected is **Sebastian Berhalter** of Vancouver Whitecaps FC, who earned his spot via fan vote following his World Cup involvement. Fellow Whitecap **Thomas Müller** was added via coach's selection, adding serious international pedigree to the Pacific Northwest club's representation.

**Rodrigo De Paul** of Inter Miami CF also features as a coach's selection at midfielder, continuing the trend of marquee international talent making their mark in MLS. Charlotte FC's **Pep Biel** and **Ashley Westwood** join them in midfield, with the host club also represented by **Tim Ream** in defense — a storyline in itself.

In attack, **Petar Musa** is among the forwards drawing attention alongside **Hugo Cuypers** (Chicago Fire FC, voted in) and **Anders Dreyer** (San Diego FC, coach's selection).

## Julian Hall: The 18-Year-Old Taking MLS by Storm

Perhaps no selection has generated more excitement than **Julian Hall** of Red Bull New York, who earns a Commissioner's Pick at just 18 years old. According to the announcement, Hall is already the **youngest hat-trick scorer in MLS history** — a staggering achievement for a teenager — and his inclusion signals just how rapidly his trajectory is climbing.

Hall's selection also reflects MLS's commitment to showcasing young domestic talent alongside established global names, reinforcing the league's dual identity as both a proving ground and a destination for top-level football.

## Tim Ream's Remarkable 15-Year All-Star Return

At the other end of the career spectrum, **Tim Ream** of host club Charlotte FC earns his All-Star nod through the fan vote — a remarkable comeback story given it reportedly marks a **15-year gap** since his previous All-Star appearance. The veteran USMNT captain's inclusion adds emotional resonance to Charlotte's inaugural All-Star hosting experience.

## Nashville SC Flexes Its Muscle With Four Selections

**Nashville SC** emerges as one of the most represented clubs, landing **four All-Stars** — goalkeeper **Brian Schwake**, defender **Andy Najar**, midfielder **Hany Mukhtar**, and goalkeeper **Matt Freese** of NYCFC rounding out a strong Eastern Conference presence. Nashville's haul reflects what reports describe as a club on pace for a historic season.

## Zavier Gozo Makes History With Dual All-Star Recognition

**Zavier Gozo** of Real Salt Lake adds another notable narrative to this year's event. According to MLS, he becomes the **first player ever named to both the MLS All-Star Game and the MLS NEXT All-Star Game**, underlining his exceptional development curve and the strength of the league's youth pipeline.

## Full Positional Breakdown

The 46-player roster is organized across all positions, with selections made through three routes: **fan vote**, **coach's selection**, and **commissioner's pick**.

- **Goalkeepers (3):** Maxime Crépeau (Orlando City SC), Matt Freese (New York City FC), Brian Schwake (Nashville SC)
- **Defenders (10):** Max Arfsten, Lucas Herrington, Richie Laryea, Anthony Markanich, Mbekezeli Mbokazi, Steven Moreira, Daniel Munie, Andy Najar, Jackson Ragen, Tim Ream
- **Midfielders (9):** Sebastian Berhalter, Pep Biel, Rodrigo De Paul, Evander, Carles Gil, Zavier Gozo, Hany Mukhtar, Thomas Müller, Ashley Westwood
- **Forwards (7, partial):** Hugo Cuypers, Anders Dreyer, Julian Hall, and four additional players confirmed in the full announcement

## What It Means for MLS at 30

This year's All-Star Game carries extra weight as MLS celebrates three decades of existence. The roster's international diversity — spanning players from World Cup squads across multiple nations — signals how dramatically the league's stature has grown on the global football stage.

Charlotte FC's role as host city adds a local dimension to the festivities, and with the All-Star jersey already available at MLSstore.com, fan engagement is already well underway ahead of the July 29 kickoff.
