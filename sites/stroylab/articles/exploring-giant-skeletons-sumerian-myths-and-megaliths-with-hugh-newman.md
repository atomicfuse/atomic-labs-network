Hugh Newman is a renowned author, explorer, and researcher who has dedicated over two decades to unraveling the secrets of megalithic sites worldwide. His passion for ancient mysteries has led him to investigate enigmatic structures and historical narratives that challenge conventional theories about human history.

Newman is the founder of the Megalithomania Conference, a prestigious event now in its 20th year. This conference serves as a platform for academics, archaeologists, and independent researchers to delve into the multifaceted aspects of ancient sites. Through this initiative, Newman fosters a collaborative environment where discoveries and theories are shared and explored.

## The World's First Megaliths

Hugh Newman co-authored the book *Gobekli Tepe and Karahan Tepe: The World's First Megaliths*, which sheds light on ancient constructions that predate Stonehenge by thousands of years. These sites in Turkey offer critical insights into the capabilities and beliefs of early civilizations, challenging the timeline of human development as we know it.

## Giants and Sumerian Myths

Newman has also delved deep into the lore of giants and Sumerian myths, topics that continue to fascinate and puzzle researchers. By examining archaeological evidence and ancient texts, he provides a fresh perspective on how these myths could be rooted in historical events or misunderstood phenomena.

## Global Explorations

Newman's fieldwork extends across the globe, with significant expeditions in Turkey, Egypt, Malta, Peru, and Britain. His hands-on approach allows him to uncover new layers of understanding about ancient earth grids and metrology. Regularly leading research tours, Newman opens doors to sites often inaccessible to the general public, offering a unique opportunity to witness history firsthand.

## Sharing Discoveries

Through appearances on Gaia's Ancient Civilizations series and his Megalithomania YouTube channel, Hugh Newman shares his findings with a wider audience. His documentation of new discoveries as they occur provides valuable contributions to the field of ancient history and archaeology.
