## High-End Jewelry Takes Center Stage at Bonhams

Bonhams New York is set to host its Exceptional Jewels auction on June 8, featuring an impressive collection of high-end diamonds and colored gemstones that could attract significant interest from luxury collectors and investors.

## Star Lot: 32.20-Carat Yellow Diamond

The auction's top lot is a stunning 32.20-carat fancy intense yellow diamond ring set in 18k white gold, carrying an estimate of $350,000 to $550,000. This exceptional piece represents the growing collector appetite for colored diamonds, which have been commanding premium prices in recent luxury auctions.

## Notable Pieces from Prestigious Houses

The sale showcases signed pieces from legendary luxury houses including Harry Winston, Cartier, and Van Cleef & Arpels. A standout Harry Winston creation features a 7.08-carat Colombian emerald and diamond ring crafted in platinum, estimated at $250,000 to $350,000.

Other significant lots include an 11.20-carat emerald-cut diamond ring in platinum ($250,000–$350,000) and an 8.23-carat faint pink diamond ring set in 18k white and pink gold ($180,000–$280,000).

## Colored Gemstones Command Attention

According to reports, the auction highlights a renewed interest in colored gemstones over traditional colorless diamonds. Among the featured colored stones is a 30.61-carat Paraíba-type tourmaline necklace in 14k gold, estimated at $150,000 to $250,000, representing the emerging collector category of rare colored stones.

A 5.02-carat Burmese ruby and diamond necklace carries an estimate of $100,000 to $150,000, while a Bulgari multicolored sapphire and diamond necklace featuring a 24.10-carat yellow sapphire is expected to fetch $125,000 to $225,000.

## Estate Collections Tell Stories of Influence

The sale features pieces from notable private collections, including items from philanthropist Glorya Kaufman and fashion pioneer Linda Dresner's estates. These estate pieces offer collectors a window into the taste and wealth of influential figures while representing the intersection of luxury collecting and heritage storytelling.

A J.E. Caldwell multicolor sapphire, zircon, and diamond necklace in platinum rounds out the high-value offerings with an estimate of $150,000 to $250,000.
