### The Rise of Food Tourism in India
In an ever-evolving world of travel, a new trend is emerging, and it's delicious! A recent survey revealed that 40% of Indian travellers are now prioritising culinary experiences while planning their trips. This movement reflects a broader desire to seek immersive and shareable experiences that go beyond the usual sightseeing. Let’s delve into how food is transforming the way Indian globetrotters decide where to go and what to eat.

### Discovering the Culinary Heart of a Destination
When planning a vacation, many Indian travellers are no longer asking, "What sights should I see?" Instead, it's shifted to, "What should I eat?" This change emphasises the increasing importance of food in creating memorable travel experiences. From street food tours to high-end dining, culinary tourism offers a more personal connection to a place.

### Best Indian Cities to Visit for Food Lovers
Curating a culinary itinerary? Here are a few Indian cities that should top your food-centric travel list:

#### 1. Delhi – A Melting Pot of Flavors
With its diverse gastronomic landscape, Delhi is a paradise for food lovers. From spicy street food in Chandni Chowk to world-class dining in Connaught Place, the city offers endless culinary delights. Don’t miss the iconic butter chicken and delectable chaat!

#### 2. Mumbai – Where Street Meets Chic
Mumbai boasts a vibrant food scene that celebrates both tradition and innovation. Whether it's devouring vada pav from a roadside stall or savoring gourmet dishes at a luxury restaurant, there's something for every palate. Be sure to try the local seafood dishes from the coastal restaurants.

#### 3. Kolkata – A City of Culinary Heritage
Known for its rich cultural heritage, Kolkata is famous for its Bengali cuisine. Treat yourself to authentic dishes like fish curry and sweets such as rasgulla. The city also offers a variety of street food, making it a food lover’s paradise.

#### 4. Hyderabad – The Biryani Capital
Hyderabad is synonymous with biryani, but there’s so much more to explore. Try the local haleem during Ramadan or indulge in a traditional thali at a local eatery. The city’s food is not just about taste; it's about a rich history too.

### Crafting a Food-First Weekend Itinerary
If you’re planning a weekend getaway focusing on food, here is a sample itinerary that you might find enticing:

**Day 1: Arrive and Explore**  
- **Morning:** Start your day with a local breakfast. Don’t forget to try the popular poha or dosa.
- **Afternoon:** Enjoy a food walking tour to sample street snacks like pani puri and samosas.
- **Evening:** Dine at a local restaurant known for its regional specialties.

**Day 2: Full-Day Culinary Adventure**  
- **Morning:** Book a cooking class to learn how to make traditional dishes.
- **Afternoon:** Visit a local market to source fresh ingredients and chat with vendors.
- **Evening:** Treat yourself to a multi-course meal at a reputed restaurant, paired with local drinks.

### Local Dishes Worth Travelling For
- **Kolkata's Puchka:** Also known as panipuri, this delectable snack is a must-try.
- **Mumbai's Pav Bhaji:** A spicy vegetable mix served with buttered bread—pure bliss!
- **Hyderabad's Dum Biryani:** A dish that tells stories of its Mughal heritage, it is an experience that one must not miss.
- **Chettinad Chicken:** Hailing from Tamil Nadu, this spicy chicken curry is packed with flavors.
