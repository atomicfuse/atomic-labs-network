As the number of global voyagers continues to increase, it's essential to comprehend the role we play in preventing the proliferation of diseases such as African Swine Fever (ASF). This highly contagious and lethal disease affects pigs, wreaking havoc on the swine industry worldwide. However, with your help, we can halt its spread.

ASF, a severe viral infection, is not a threat to human health, but it decimates pig populations. There's no known cure or vaccine for this disease presently, making preventive measures crucial. The global swine industry is heavily impacted by ASF, with significant economic implications.

## How can you help?

When packing for international travel, it's important to avoid carrying pork products. Even seemingly harmless items like ham sandwiches can carry the ASF virus. In addition, travelers should avoid visiting farms or areas with pigs, especially in regions where ASF is prevalent. If you have to visit, it's crucial to clean and disinfect shoes and clothes before and after the visit.

## Importance of Biosecurity

Biosecurity procedures play a crucial role in controlling ASF. These measures are designed to prevent the introduction and spread of diseases by controlling and eliminating risk factors. Adhering to strict biosecurity protocols in pig farms and slaughterhouses can help control ASF.

## Protecting Domestic Swine

Particularly in countries with large pig farming sectors, travelers returning from ASF-affected countries should avoid contact with pigs for at least five days. This precautionary measure is critical in preventing the disease from crossing borders and infecting domestic swine.

## Reporting Suspected Cases

If you suspect ASF infection in a pig, it's vital to report it to local animal health authorities immediately. Quick actions can prevent the disease from spreading, saving countless pigs and mitigating economic losses for pig farmers.

Awareness and diligence in following these guidelines can help protect our global swine population from ASF. Remember, your actions can make a significant difference. Together, we can join forces to fight ASF and ensure the health and wellbeing of our global pig industry.
