## Swedish Summer Accommodations for Every Travel Style

As Midsummer travel to Scandinavia reaches its peak in June, according to reports, Vogue has curated nine stylish Airbnb rentals across Sweden that cater to different travel preferences. Each property offers a unique experience during the extended summer daylight hours that make Swedish summers so distinctive.

## Coastal and Waterfront Properties

For travelers seeking a seaside experience, the Österlen House stands out as an affordable option at $157 per night. This coastal accommodation provides the perfect base for experiencing Sweden's maritime culture during the Midsummer season.

Another waterfront option, Kungshamn, also priced at $157 per night, features contemporary design elements that showcase modern Scandinavian aesthetics. The property offers travelers a chance to experience Sweden's renowned design philosophy while enjoying waterfront views.

## Historic and Cultural Accommodations

History enthusiasts can choose the Gamla Kassan at $185 per night, which caters specifically to those interested in Sweden's cultural heritage. For those seeking a truly unique experience, the Beautiful Chapel offers accommodation in a renovated 1912 Baptist church located on the island of Gotland, providing both historical significance and island charm.

In Nyköping, the Charming Cottage presents an authentic 18th-century experience, allowing guests to stay in a property that predates modern Sweden by centuries.

## Design-Focused and Luxury Options

The Blombergsgården, priced at $164 per night, specifically targets travelers seeking design inspiration. This property showcases the clean lines and functional beauty that have made Scandinavian design internationally celebrated.

For those with larger budgets, the House with a Fantastic View commands $334 per night but offers proximity to Stockholm, making it ideal for travelers who want to combine capital city exploration with a stylish countryside retreat.

## Remote and Secluded Experiences

The Aurora Isle, at $183 per night, provides what reports describe as "true seclusion." This private island cabin offers an off-the-grid experience that appeals to travelers seeking minimalist living during their Swedish adventure.

For a more accessible but still peaceful option, the Cosy House is located outside Stockholm's center, providing a quieter alternative to city accommodation while maintaining reasonable access to urban attractions.
