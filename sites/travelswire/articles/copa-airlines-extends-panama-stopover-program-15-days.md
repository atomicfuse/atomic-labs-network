## Copa Airlines Doubles Panama Stopover Duration

Copa Airlines has significantly expanded its Panama Stopover program, extending the maximum duration from seven days to 15 days at no additional airfare cost. This enhancement allows travelers to transform what would typically be a brief layover into an extended exploration of Panama City and the surrounding region.

## Strategic Tourism Push for Hub Traffic

The expansion represents Panama's strategic effort to convert its substantial connection traffic into actual visitors. According to reports, Copa is targeting 250,000 stopover visitors in 2026, positioning Panama as an accessible two-destination trip for travelers moving between North and South America.

This initiative reflects a broader industry trend where airlines and tourism boards are working to transform hub cities from mere layover inconveniences into attractive bonus destinations. The stopover program capitalizes on Panama City's position as a major connection point for inter-American travel.

## How the Program Works

The Panama Stopover program allows eligible travelers to extend their stay in Panama City without paying additional airfare costs. Instead of rushing through a traditional layover, passengers can spend up to 15 days exploring the country before continuing to their final destination.

This approach particularly benefits points-and-miles enthusiasts who use Copa Airlines, United MileagePlus, or other Star Alliance partnerships, as it maximizes value from existing award bookings or paid tickets.

## Panama's Appeal Beyond the Airport

Panama offers diverse attractions that justify extended stopovers. The country features pristine beaches along both Pacific and Caribbean coasts, extensive rainforest areas rich with biodiversity, and a vibrant cultural scene centered in Panama City's historic Casco Viejo district.

The capital city combines modern skyline views with colonial architecture, while the famous Panama Canal provides insights into one of the world's most significant engineering achievements. These attractions make Panama more than just a connection hub.

## Flexible Itinerary Options

The extended 15-day window accommodates various travel styles and interests. Travelers can design shorter three- to five-day urban explorations focusing on Panama City's restaurants, museums, and cultural sites. Those preferring week-long adventures can venture into national parks or coastal regions.

The full 15-day option enables comprehensive exploration, combining city experiences with nature excursions, beach relaxation, and cultural immersion. This flexibility addresses different traveler preferences while maintaining the program's core value proposition of no additional airfare costs.

## Growing Stopover Trend

Copa's program expansion aligns with increasing industry recognition of stopover programs' effectiveness. Airlines worldwide are discovering that hub cities can become revenue generators rather than operational necessities, creating win-win scenarios for carriers, destinations, and travelers.

These programs particularly appeal to travelers seeking maximum value from their airfare investments, especially those comfortable with slightly more complex itineraries in exchange for experiencing additional destinations.

## Planning Your Panama Stopover

Travelers interested in the extended stopover program should consider Panama's tropical climate when planning activities and packing. The country's compact size makes it possible to experience multiple environments within the 15-day window, from urban sophistication to pristine wilderness.

The program's success will likely depend on travelers' awareness and willingness to embrace slightly more complex travel arrangements in exchange for experiencing Panama's diverse offerings at no additional airfare cost.
