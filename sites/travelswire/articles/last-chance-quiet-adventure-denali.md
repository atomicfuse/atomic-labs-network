## TL;DR
This summer offers a rare opportunity to explore Denali National Park's backcountry with minimal crowds before the Park Road reopens in 2027. Discover how to access the Kantishna area, the benefits of staying at unique lodges, and the impact of climate change on national park access.

## A Unique Window of Opportunity
If you’ve ever dreamed of experiencing the breathtaking beauty of Denali National Park with hardly anyone around, this is your chance! Due to a landslide closure and climate change impacting the region, the iconic Park Road won't reopen until 2027. So, if you're looking for an extraordinary escape into the wilderness, now is the time to visit — it may never be this quiet again.

## Exploring the Kantishna Area
While the Park Road is off-limits beyond mile 43, the remote Kantishna area is still accessible via backpacking or by flying into one of three incredible lodges. Imagine immersing yourself in the stunning wilderness where the towering peaks and serene landscapes are all yours to explore without encountering the usual crowds.

### The Three Lodges
Denali offers three unique fly-in lodges, each providing different experiences and access points into the park's stunning backcountry. Let’s break them down:

#### 1. Kantishna Roadhouse
Kantishna Roadhouse is your home for comfort in the wild. Surrounded by breathtaking landscapes and only a stone's throw away from Denali's natural wonders, this lodge provides cozy accommodations, hearty meals, and guided excursions. You can enjoy activities like hiking, mountain biking, and even gold panning, making it a fantastic choice for families and those seeking a balance of adventure and comfort.

#### 2. Denali Backcountry Lodge
For those looking for a more rustic experience, Denali Backcountry Lodge offers intimate cabins with an emphasis on sustainability. The lodge provides delicious farm-to-table dining and endless opportunities for outdoor exploration, including nature walks, wildlife spotting, and guided hikes into Denali's serene wilderness. 

#### 3. Camp Denali
A more intimate and immersive experience can be found at Camp Denali, where guests are accommodated in comfortable cabins nestled in nature. With a strong focus on environmental education, Camp Denali emphasizes sustainable practices and hosts various activities led by knowledgeable naturalists. It's perfect for those seeking a deep connection with the land, alongside cozy comfort. 

## How To Access Denali’s Hidden Gems
Don’t let the closed Park Road deter you! You can still access Denali National Park’s interior through one of the lodges' flight services, which transport guests over stunning backcountry terrain, providing a picturesque view of the 49-mile section of road that’s currently closed. Alternatively, consider embarking on a backpacking adventure for the more adventurous travelers. Foot access may require some planning and experience, but the rewards are immeasurable!
