One of the biggest changes for the 2026 tournament is the expansion from 32 to **48 teams**. This means more matches, more excitement, and more opportunities for upsets! The tournament will kick off with a group stage where teams will be divided into 16 groups of three. ### How the Knockout Stage Works
Once in the knockout stage, the competition heats up. The **Round of 32** will feature the qualifying teams facing off in single-elimination matches. Each match is crucial—win or go home! Here’s how the knockout process will work:
- **Round of 32**: The top two teams from each group proceed to this round.
- **Round of 16**: Winners from the Round of 32 will advance.
- **Quarterfinals**: The remaining teams will continue until only four remain.
- **Semifinals and Finals**: Finally, the title will be contested between the last two standing teams.

### Tiebreakers and Third-Place Teams
In this unique format, what happens if teams are level on points? Fear not! The tiebreaker rules will apply as follows:
1. Goal difference
2. Goals scored
3. Head-to-head results
4. Fair play points
5. Finally, a drawing of lots if all else fails.

Additionally, excitingly, **third-place teams** from the groups can still advance. This opens up more drama and allows teams that may not top their groups to continue their World Cup journey.
