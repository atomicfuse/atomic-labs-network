## The Biggest World Cup Ever Is Coming — Here's How to Watch Without Paying Full Price

The 2026 FIFA World Cup is shaping up to be the largest in tournament history, featuring 104 matches played across North America. With that many games on the calendar, fans are already hunting for the most flexible and affordable ways to catch every kick. The good news: there are several legitimate options — many of them free or low-cost — available to viewers in the United States.

## Where to Watch: Broadcast and Streaming Channels

The 2026 World Cup will be broadcast across a range of English and Spanish-language platforms. According to reports, **FOX** and **FS1** are the primary English-language broadcast homes for the tournament, while **Telemundo** and **Universo** are carrying matches in Spanish.

For cord-cutters and streaming-first fans, options include:

- **Peacock** — NBCUniversal's streaming platform
- **FOX One** — FOX's own streaming app
- **YouTube TV** — a live TV streaming service
- **DirecTV** — available via satellite or its streaming tier
- **Fubo** — a sports-focused live TV streaming platform
- **Over-the-air antenna** — FOX and Telemundo are both available free over antenna in most U.S. markets

For fans who want to spend as little as possible, an antenna paired with a free-trial strategy across streaming services may be the most budget-friendly route.

## Free Trial Strategy for Cord-Cutters

For fans who don't want to commit to a full subscription, free trials offered by services like YouTube TV, Fubo, and DirecTV's streaming tier can be timed strategically around marquee match weeks. Signing up at the start of a key group-stage week and managing trial windows could allow fans to watch several rounds of matches at no cost.

Over-the-air antennas remain one of the most reliable and permanently free options, since both FOX and Telemundo broadcast over local airwaves in most major U.S. cities.

## English vs. Spanish Viewing Guide

Fans watching in English will primarily turn to FOX, FS1, and FOX One. Those who prefer Spanish-language commentary should look to Telemundo and its streaming partner Universo, which are also available via antenna and most cable or streaming TV bundles.

## Planning Ahead: Watch Parties and Travel

With 104 matches spread across North American host cities, demand for watch party venues, travel packages, and fan zone access is already rising. Fans planning to attend or host viewing events are encouraged to map out the schedule early, particularly around high-demand fixtures like the United States, Brazil, Argentina, France, and England group-stage openers.
