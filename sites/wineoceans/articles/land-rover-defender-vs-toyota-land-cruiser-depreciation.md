## Luxury Off-Road Icons Show Vastly Different Resale Performance

Recent depreciation analysis reveals a significant gap between two iconic off-road SUVs, with the Land Rover Defender losing value much faster than the Toyota Land Cruiser despite commanding a higher starting price.

According to CarEdge estimates, the Defender faces five-year depreciation of 48%, while the Land Cruiser depreciates just 35% over the same period. This 13-percentage-point difference represents thousands of dollars in lost value for Defender owners.

## The Real Cost of Luxury SUV Ownership

The depreciation gap highlights the hidden costs of luxury SUV ownership that extend well beyond the initial purchase price. Large luxury SUVs are increasingly scrutinized for their total ownership costs, making resale value a major consideration for potential buyers.

While the Defender carries prestige and appeals to luxury off-road buyers, the Land Cruiser emerges as the stronger long-term value proposition according to the analysis. This creates a compelling case for buyers to look beyond brand image when evaluating these off-road capable vehicles.

## Toyota's Resale Value Advantage

The data reinforces Toyota's reputation for producing vehicles that retain their value better than many competitors. The Land Cruiser's superior resale performance suggests that Toyota SUVs maintain stronger market demand in the used vehicle segment compared to Land Rover offerings.

This trend has implications for buyers considering either new or used versions of these vehicles. The steep depreciation curve of the Defender could present opportunities for used SUV buyers willing to let someone else absorb the initial value loss.

## Strategic Buying Considerations

For buyers focused on total cost of ownership, the five to seven-year ownership picture favors the Land Cruiser significantly. However, the Defender's rapid depreciation creates a potential buying opportunity for those willing to purchase used vehicles after the steepest depreciation has already occurred.

The analysis suggests that buyers seeking luxury off-road capability might benefit from waiting to purchase a depreciated Defender rather than buying new, while those prioritizing long-term value retention should consider the Land Cruiser's proven resale strength.
