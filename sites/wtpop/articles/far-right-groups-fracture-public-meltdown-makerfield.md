## Conspiracy Theories Replace Strategic Planning

According to reports, conspiracy theories have become the primary vehicle for explaining away the movement's failures in Makerfield. Rather than conducting honest assessments of their electoral strategy or messaging, competing factions have resorted to elaborate theories about betrayal and sabotage within their own ranks.

## Social Media Amplifies the Chaos

Reports indicate that social media platforms are playing a significant role in amplifying and accelerating the extremist infighting. The public nature of these disputes is particularly damaging to the far-right's attempts to present itself as a serious political force. This disconnect between digital influence and real-world political power has forced uncomfortable questions about the movement's actual size and appeal. The resulting cognitive dissonance appears to be driving much of the current conspiracy theorizing and blame-shifting.

The Makerfield aftermath appears to confirm this analysis, as ideological purity tests and personal loyalty disputes take precedence over strategic thinking.
