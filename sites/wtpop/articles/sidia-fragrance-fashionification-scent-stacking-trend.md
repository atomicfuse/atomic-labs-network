## The Rise of Scent Stacking

Fragrance is undergoing a major transformation from a single signature scent to a fashion-forward wardrobe staple, with consumers increasingly embracing "scent stacking"—the practice of layering multiple fragrance products to create personalized scent combinations. This shift represents a fundamental change in how younger generations approach personal fragrance.

According to reports, Pinterest has identified scent stacking as a defining beauty trend for 2026, with searches for "body mist perfume" surging an impressive 1,648% year-over-year. Additionally, creator conversations around scent identity have risen 47%, signaling a generational shift in fragrance consumption patterns.

## Sidia's Strategic Expansion

At the forefront of this movement is Sidia, a fragrance and body care brand founded by Erin Kleinberg. The brand is capitalizing on the scent stacking trend and expanding into Sephora Canada this summer, marking a significant milestone in its growth trajectory.

Sidia's approach exemplifies what industry observers are calling the "fashionification" of fragrance—the evolution of scent from a beauty category into a fashion accessory. The brand's products, including offerings called Braless and Midas, are positioned as everyday luxury items that consumers can mix and match like clothing pieces.

## A New Approach to Fragrance Identity

The traditional concept of having one signature scent is giving way to a more dynamic approach among Gen Z and millennial consumers. According to reports, these demographics are increasingly using fragrance as a form of personal expression, treating scent selection with the same intentionality as outfit building.

This shift reflects a broader psychology around fragrance identity, where consumers use different scents to express various versions of themselves throughout the day. The practice allows for greater creativity and personalization in how individuals present themselves to the world.

## The Role of Portable Formats

The trend toward scent stacking has been facilitated by the rise of portable fragrance formats, including solid perfumes and body mists in glass bottles. These formats make it easier for consumers to carry multiple fragrances and layer them as desired, supporting the wardrobe-like approach to fragrance.

Body mists, in particular, have gained significant traction as a more accessible entry point into fragrance layering, offering lighter scent options that can be combined with stronger perfumes or used alone for subtle fragrance moments.

## Building Cultural Credibility

Sidia's founder-led approach demonstrates a strategic focus on building cultural credibility through independent retailers before pursuing major retail expansion. This methodology contrasts with traditional beauty brand strategies that might prioritize immediate retail milestones over cultural touchpoints.

The brand's expansion into Sephora Canada represents the culmination of this approach, suggesting that cultural authenticity and consumer education around scent stacking have created sufficient demand to warrant major retail partnerships.

## Tutorial and Educational Opportunities

The scent stacking trend has created numerous educational opportunities for both brands and content creators. Tutorial content focusing on how to layer body mists, oils, and perfumes for different moods and occasions has become increasingly popular among beauty and lifestyle audiences.

This educational aspect is crucial to the trend's continued growth, as consumers need guidance on how to effectively combine different fragrance products without creating overwhelming or conflicting scent profiles.

## The Future of Fragrance

As the "fashionification" of fragrance continues to evolve, brands like Sidia are positioning themselves at the intersection of beauty and fashion. The trend suggests that future fragrance consumption will be characterized by versatility, personalization, and the same seasonal and occasion-based considerations that drive fashion purchases.

The significant growth in related search terms and creator content indicates that this shift represents more than a passing trend—it appears to be a fundamental change in how consumers approach fragrance as part of their daily routine and personal style expression.
