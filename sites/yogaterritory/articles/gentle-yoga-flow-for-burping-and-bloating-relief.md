Feeling bloated or struggling with trapped air in your chest or belly can be uncomfortable. Luckily, yoga offers a natural way to support digestion and encourage the release of trapped air. This 20-minute gentle yoga flow is perfect for those seeking relief from gas and bloating through mindful movement.

This routine focuses on simple poses and movements such as twists, side stretches, and chest-opening exercises to help your body move air upward, leaving you feeling lighter and more at ease. No prior yoga experience is required—just a mat and a willingness to breathe deeply.

## Encouraging Natural Burping

Burping is a natural process that helps expel air from the stomach. This yoga flow incorporates specific movements designed to encourage upward air release. Twists and side stretches play a crucial role here, as they gently massage the internal organs and promote the movement of trapped gas.

## Relieving Pressure from Gas and Bloating

Gas and bloating can cause significant discomfort. This yoga sequence includes poses that apply gentle pressure on the abdomen, aiding in digestion and relieving the sensation of fullness. Movements like seated belly breath and wind-relieving pose are particularly effective in easing pressure.

## Improving Digestion

Good digestion is key to feeling comfortable and balanced. By practicing mindful movement, this yoga flow enhances digestion by stimulating the gastrointestinal tract. Poses like cat-cow and spinal rocks help in creating a rhythm that aids in the digestive process.

## Practice Anytime

This short practice fits perfectly into your morning or evening routine. Whether you wake up feeling bloated or experience gas throughout the day, dedicating 20 minutes to this flow can make a significant difference. It's a simple yet effective way to connect with your body and promote overall well-being.

Join this journey towards feeling lighter and more relaxed, and remember that consistent practice can lead to lasting benefits. Embrace the gentle movements and enjoy the transformation they bring.
