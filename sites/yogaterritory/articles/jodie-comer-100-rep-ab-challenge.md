## What You Need to Know: Jodie Comer's 100-Rep Challenge
The beauty of Jodie’s 100-rep ab challenge is its simplicity and efficiency. This workout consists of five movements, each performed 20 times, challenging your core in various ways. Here’s a breakdown of the routine:

### 1. 20 Crunches
- **How to do it:** Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground. Place your hands behind your head, engage your core, and lift your shoulders towards your knees, squeezing at the top before lowering back down.
- **Tip:** Avoid pulling on your neck - let your abs do the work!

### 2. 20 Toe Taps, Legs Together
- **How to do it:** Lie on your back, lift your legs to a tabletop position (knees bent at 90 degrees). Slowly lower one foot to tap the floor while keeping the other elevated, and alternate.
- **Modification:** If this is too challenging, keep one foot flat on the ground. 

### 3. 20 Toe Taps, Left Side
- **How to do it:** From the previous position, focus on tapping one foot down to the ground on your left side, then return to the starting position. Repeat with the right side.

### 4. 20 Toe Taps, Right Side
- **Repeat the same motion for the right side.** This will help in engaging your obliques and targeting side abdominal muscles.

### 5. 20 Toe Taps, Legs Together
- **Repeat the first toe tap exercise but with legs together.** This final round strengthens your core while maintaining control and stability.

## Modifications for Beginners
If you’re new to core workouts, don’t worry! Here are a few beginner modifications to help you ease into Jodie’s challenge:
- **Crunches:** Instead of raising both shoulders off the ground, try lifting just one shoulder and then alternate.
- **Toe Taps:** Keep your feet elevated at all times until you feel comfortable lowering them. Alternatively, you can keep your head and shoulders resting on the mat during toe taps.

## Why Pilates-Specific Workouts? 
Jodie’s challenge incorporates principles of Pilates, which emphasizes controlled movements and proper form over quantity. Unlike traditional sit-ups, which can strain your back if done incorrectly, Pilates engages multiple muscle groups safely and effectively, promoting a more balanced and stronger core.

## Challenge Yourself: The 5-Round Margot Robbie Progression
Once you're comfortable with the basics, try to stage a progression for an added challenge! For instance, aim to increase your repetitions each week or incorporate a side plank after each round. You might find yourself crushing that core in no time!

## Common Form Mistakes to Avoid
To maximize your results and minimize injury risks, keep an eye on your form:
- Avoid overarching your back during crunches, which can lead to discomfort.
- During toe taps, keep your heels lifted off the floor and your core engaged.
