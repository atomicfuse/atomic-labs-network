## TL;DR
If you’re looking to improve flexibility and ease tension from sitting all day, try these five beginner-friendly yoga poses at home. They’re perfect for releasing stiffness and enhancing mobility!

## Introduction
Feeling stiff and tense after a long day at the desk? You’re not alone! With our increasingly sedentary lifestyles, many of us are experiencing tight hips, shoulders, and backs. The good news is that you don’t need to be an advanced yogi to relieve this discomfort. In fact, simple at-home yoga can dramatically improve your flexibility and overall mobility without any extreme stretching. Let’s dive into five beginner-friendly yoga poses that will help you feel more relaxed and limber in no time!

## 1. Cat-Cow Pose
### Move with your breath
Cat-Cow is a wonderful way to warm up your spine and release built-up tension. 

**How to do it:**  
1. Start on all fours with your wrists under your shoulders and knees under your hips.  
2. Inhale as you drop your belly and lift your head and tailbone toward the sky (Cow Pose).  
3. Exhale as you arch your back and tuck your chin to your chest (Cat Pose).  
4. Repeat this flow for several breaths, moving gently and rhythmically.  

**Benefits:**  
This pose improves flexibility in your spine and alleviates stress, making it great for those long days of sitting.

## 2. Downward-Facing Dog
### A full-body stretch
Downward-Facing Dog is a go-to yoga pose that stretches your entire body, especially your hamstrings and calves.

**How to do it:**  
1. From the all-fours position, tuck your toes and lift your hips up and back to form an inverted V shape.  
2. Keep your spine straight and your heels reaching toward the ground.  
3. Press your palms firmly into the mat and breathe deeply for 5 breaths.  

**Benefits:**  
This pose not only stretches your legs and spine, but it also strengthens your arms and shoulders, making it a fantastic way to unwind.

## 3. Low Lunge
### Great for hips and thighs
The Low Lunge helps open the hips and stretches the thighs. It’s perfect for individuals who sit for long periods.

**How to do it:**  
1. From Downward-Facing Dog, bring your right foot forward between your hands.  
2. Lower your left knee to the floor and press your hips forward gently.  
3. Hold for several breaths, then switch sides.  

**Benefits:**  
This pose helps release tightness in the hip flexors and improves hip mobility, which is key for a comfortable daily movement.

## 4. Cobra Pose
### Lift your chest and strengthen your back
Cobra Pose opens the chest and strengthens your spine, combating the hunch from hours at the computer.

**How to do it:**  
1. Lie flat on your stomach with your hands under your shoulders.  
2. Inhale as you press through your hands, lifting your chest off the ground.  
3. Keep your elbows slightly bent and shoulders away from your ears.  
4. Hold for 3–5 breaths, then lower back down.  

**Benefits:**  
This pose counteracts sitting posture and enhances spinal flexibility, giving your back the love it deserves.

## 5. Child’s Pose
### A gentle restorative pose
Finally, Child’s Pose is a comforting resting pose that helps to soothe the mind while stretching the back and hips.

**How to do it:**  
1. Kneel on the floor and sit back on your heels.  
2. Inhale and stretch your arms forward, lowering your chest toward the mat.  
3. Relax your forehead down and breathe deeply, staying for as long as feels good.  

**Benefits:**  
Child’s Pose invites relaxation and mindfulness, making it a perfect way to end your practice or take a break during your busy day.

## Conclusion
Incorporating these five beginner-friendly yoga poses into your routine can greatly improve your flexibility and reduce the stiffness caused by our modern lifestyles. Remember, the goal isn’t to achieve complex poses, but to embrace your body and find ease through movement. So roll out your mat and give these poses a try at home—you might find a newfound sense of freedom in your body and mind! 💪🧘‍♀️
